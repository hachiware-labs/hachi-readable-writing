# Proposal: Later Library Hours on Tuesdays and Thursdays

Good morning. I am asking the operations meeting to approve a three-month trial of later library hours, from June through August, along with a ¥420,000 budget for the additional security and utility costs.

The proposal is straightforward: keep the library open until 8:00 p.m. on Tuesdays and Thursdays. This is not a proposal to extend every day, and it does not change the services available throughout the building. It is a focused trial designed to test whether two later weekday evenings meet a demonstrated need within a manageable staffing and cost limit.

## Why consider later hours now?

Our current public hours are 9:00 a.m. to 6:00 p.m., Tuesday through Sunday. For many residents, especially people who work during the day or attend school, a 6:00 p.m. closing time makes weekday use difficult.

The March user survey gives us a clear signal. Of 320 respondents, 62 percent said they wanted weekday access after 7:00 p.m. The strongest demand came from office workers and high-school students. These are residents who may not be able to visit during daytime hours but could use the library for reading, borrowing materials, returns, and quiet study in the evening.

Our entry data points in the same direction. During weekdays in April, an average of 84 people entered the library between 5:00 and 6:00 p.m. The daytime hourly average was 46. In other words, the final open hour of the day already draws substantially more visitors than a typical daytime hour.

That does not guarantee that people will stay or arrive after 6:00 p.m. But it is strong enough evidence to justify a limited trial. The question before us is not whether every weekday should have longer hours. The question is whether two evenings a week can give these users meaningful access while remaining operationally sustainable.

## The proposed trial

The library would remain open until 8:00 p.m. on Tuesdays and Thursdays, from June through August.

The late period would offer a deliberately limited service. After 6:00 p.m., the first-floor reading area, borrowing services, and returns would remain available. The children’s area and meeting rooms would close at 6:00 p.m.

This scope matters. It concentrates the evening service on the activities most likely to serve workers, students, and other residents who need access outside standard hours, while keeping the trial practical to staff and supervise.

The proposal is also limited in duration. A three-month period lets us observe use across a meaningful number of weekday evenings without committing the library to a permanent schedule before we have evidence.

## Staffing and operating impact

We do not have staffing capacity to extend hours every day. That is why this proposal is limited to Tuesdays and Thursdays.

Each late shift would include two staff members and one security guard. Existing shifts would be rearranged, and total overtime is expected to remain within 24 hours per month.

The additional security and utility costs for the full three-month trial are estimated at ¥420,000. This is the budget approval requested today.

The proposal does not assume that a successful trial will be cost-free or effortless. It sets a defined financial boundary and a limited staffing arrangement, then asks us to measure the actual impact rather than rely only on expectations.

## How we will evaluate the trial

During June through August, we will record visitor numbers and loans by time of day, as well as costs and staff workload.

These measures will answer the practical questions we need to resolve:

- Are residents using the library after 6:00 p.m., and on which evenings?
- Are they using borrowing and return services as well as the reading area?
- Do the additional costs remain within the approved budget?
- Can the rearranged shifts and late staffing arrangement be sustained without excessive workload?
- Does the reduced evening service operate safely?

Continuation after August would require three conditions.

First, the library must receive at least 50 visitors per evening after 6:00 p.m. This threshold ensures that we are responding to regular, meaningful demand rather than isolated peaks.

Second, there must be no serious safety problems. The trial must be useful without compromising the safety of visitors or staff.

Third, costs must remain within budget. A later-hours service must be operationally responsible as well as publicly valuable.

If those conditions are not met, we will have clear evidence for adjusting or ending the extended-hours arrangement. If they are met, the meeting will have a sound basis for considering continuation.

## Decision requested

The evidence shows a real access gap: a majority of surveyed users want weekday access after 7:00 p.m., demand is particularly strong among office workers and high-school students, and weekday use already rises sharply in the hour before closing.

At the same time, our staffing limits mean that a broad extension would not be appropriate. The proposed Tuesday and Thursday trial is a proportionate response: limited in scope, limited in duration, and designed to produce the data needed for a longer-term decision.

I ask the meeting to approve the June-through-August trial of 8:00 p.m. closing on Tuesdays and Thursdays, and to approve the ¥420,000 budget for additional security and utility costs.
