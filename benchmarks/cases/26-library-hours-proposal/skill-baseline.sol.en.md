# Proposal for Extended Library Hours

Good morning. Today, I am asking the operations meeting to approve a three-month trial of extended evening hours at the city library, together with a budget of ¥420,000.

The proposal is straightforward: from June through August, the library would remain open until 8:00 p.m. on Tuesdays and Thursdays. This limited trial would respond to demonstrated demand while allowing us to evaluate attendance, cost, safety, and staff workload before considering any permanent change.

## Why Later Access Is Needed

The library is currently open from 9:00 a.m. to 6:00 p.m., Tuesday through Sunday. These hours provide broad daytime access, but they make weekday visits difficult for people whose work or school day ends in the late afternoon.

Our March survey gives us a clear indication of this problem. Of the 320 users surveyed, 62 percent said they wanted weekday access after 7:00 p.m. Demand was strongest among office workers and high-school students. These are two groups that may value the library’s services but have limited opportunities to arrive before the current 6:00 p.m. closing time.

Actual visitor numbers support the survey findings. On weekdays in April, an average of 84 people entered the library between 5:00 and 6:00 p.m. By comparison, the average for a daytime hour was 46 visitors. In other words, the final hour of the weekday schedule was substantially busier than a typical daytime hour.

This does not tell us exactly how many people would stay or arrive after 6:00 p.m. It does, however, show that demand remains strong as closing time approaches. A controlled trial is the best way to determine whether that demand continues into the evening.

## The Proposed Trial

Under the proposal, the library would stay open until 8:00 p.m. every Tuesday and Thursday for three months, from June through August.

We are proposing two evenings per week because staffing is not available for an extension every day. Tuesdays and Thursdays would provide regular, predictable access while keeping the trial within our operational capacity. Users would know that they have two weekday evenings when they can visit after work or school.

The service available after 6:00 p.m. would be intentionally limited. The children’s area and meeting rooms would close at the usual time. Evening visitors could use the first-floor reading area and borrowing and return services. Restricting access to the first floor would make the building easier to supervise and would focus staffing on the services most likely to be needed during these additional hours.

The late shift would consist of two library staff members and one security guard. We would rearrange existing shifts rather than simply add overtime to the current schedule. Total overtime is expected to remain within 24 hours per month.

This arrangement recognizes both the demand for later access and the practical limits on staffing. It is not a full expansion of all library operations. It is a measured test of whether core services can be offered safely and sustainably on two evenings each week.

## Cost and Operational Controls

The additional cost for security and utilities would total ¥420,000 over the three-month trial. Approval of that amount is part of today’s request.

The trial design includes several controls to keep the extension manageable. Only one floor would remain in use after 6:00 p.m. Two staff members would provide library services, while a security guard would support building safety. Existing shifts would be rearranged, and overtime would be monitored against the expected limit of 24 hours per month.

These controls matter because public demand alone is not enough to justify a permanent extension. We also need evidence that evening operations can remain safe, affordable, and reasonable for staff.

For that reason, the trial would collect information in four areas: visitor numbers by time, loans by time, actual operating costs, and staff workload. These measurements would allow us to distinguish between people who use the building for reading and study, people who borrow materials, and the operational effort required to support them.

## How We Will Decide What Happens Next

Before the trial begins, we will establish clear conditions for continuation. Extended hours would be considered successful only if all three conditions are met.

First, at least 50 visitors must use the library after 6:00 p.m. on each trial evening. This threshold will show whether evening access serves a substantial number of residents.

Second, there must be no serious safety problems. The presence of a security guard and the restriction to the first floor are intended to reduce risk, but the trial must confirm that the arrangement works in practice.

Third, actual costs must remain within the approved budget. We will also review staff workload and overtime so that any recommendation reflects the operational impact, not attendance alone.

At the end of August, the recorded results will give us a sound basis for a further decision. If the attendance threshold is reached, operations remain safe, and costs stay within budget, we can consider continuing or refining the schedule. If those conditions are not met, the trial can end without committing the library to a permanent extension.

## Request for Approval

Later weekday access is a consistent request from library users, particularly office workers and high-school students. The survey shows broad interest, and the high number of visitors during the current final hour suggests that demand extends beyond the daytime schedule.

At the same time, we do not yet have enough evidence to recommend a permanent change. The proposed trial addresses that uncertainty. It offers meaningful evening access twice a week, limits the area and services in operation, works within available staffing, and sets measurable standards for continuation.

I therefore ask the operations meeting to approve two actions: the extension of library hours until 8:00 p.m. on Tuesdays and Thursdays from June through August, and a budget of ¥420,000 for the additional security and utility costs.

With this approval, we can test later access under controlled conditions and return with evidence for a responsible long-term decision.
