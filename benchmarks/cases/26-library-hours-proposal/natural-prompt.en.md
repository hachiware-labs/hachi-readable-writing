Create a presentation script for the operations meeting proposing that the city library stay open later two days a week.
The presentation should take about ten minutes.

What we know:

- Current hours are 9:00 a.m. to 6:00 p.m., Tuesday through Sunday.
- A March survey of 320 users found that 62 percent wanted weekday access after 7:00 p.m.
- Demand was strongest among office workers and high-school students.
- On weekdays in April, an average of 84 people entered between 5:00 and 6:00 p.m.; the daytime hourly average was 46.
- Staffing is not available for an extension every day.
- The proposal is to remain open until 8:00 p.m. on Tuesdays and Thursdays.
- The trial would run for three months, June through August.
- The late shift would have two staff members and one security guard. Existing shifts would be rearranged, with total overtime expected to remain within 24 hours per month.
- Additional security and utility costs would total ¥420,000 for three months.
- The children's area and meeting rooms would close at 6:00 p.m. After 6:00, only first-floor reading, borrowing, and returns would be available.
- During the trial, we will record visitors and loans by time, cost, and staff workload.
- Continuation would require at least 50 visitors per evening after 6:00, no serious safety problems, and costs within budget.
- The meeting should approve the trial and its ¥420,000 budget.
