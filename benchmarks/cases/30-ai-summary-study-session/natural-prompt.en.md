Create a presentation script for an internal study session on reviewing AI-generated meeting summaries.
It is a 15-minute talk for employees who have recently started using AI in their daily work.

What we know:

- Employees increasingly use AI to summarize transcripts of online meetings.
- We reviewed 50 summaries from last month's trial.
- Six contained errors in important facts such as dates, amounts, or owners.
- Four attributed statements to the wrong speaker, and five presented unresolved matters as decisions.
- A summary could contain more than one problem.
- The prose sounded natural, which made errors hard to notice.
- The source transcript can also contain speech-recognition errors.
- Reviewers should compare decisions, deadlines, owners, numbers, and open issues with the original remarks.
- They should also check whether important objections or conditions were omitted.
- One example said, “We will introduce it next month,” when the actual remark was, “We will consider whether a trial can begin next month.”
- The person responsible for the meeting reviews the summary before it is shared with participants.
- The document records the reviewer's name and review date so readers can see that it was checked.
- Existing internal information-management rules still apply to recordings and transcripts.
- The goal is not to discourage AI use, but to explain how to use the output safely as a draft.
