# Family Discussion Memo: Repair or Replace the Refrigerator?

## Decision

Based on the money currently in the appliance savings fund, repairing the refrigerator is the only option that fits the budget while preserving the ¥80,000 needed for next month’s school-related payment.

The replacement should become the priority if the refrigerator stops cooling.

## What we know

The refrigerator is nine years old. It still cools, but it intermittently makes a loud operating noise.

- Repair: ¥38,000, including a 90-day warranty on the repaired part. Its remaining lifespan is unknown.
- Replacement: ¥148,000, including delivery and removal of the old refrigerator, with a five-year warranty.
- Estimated electricity savings from replacement: ¥1,500 per month. This is an estimate, not a guarantee.
- Appliance savings fund: ¥220,000.
- School-related payment due next month from the same fund: ¥80,000.
- Borrowing and installment payments are not options.

## Budget impact

| Choice | Remaining after purchase | After the ¥80,000 school payment |
|---|---:|---:|
| Repair | ¥182,000 | ¥102,000 |
| Replace | ¥72,000 | −¥8,000 |

Replacing the refrigerator would therefore leave the fund ¥8,000 short of the school payment, based on the funds currently identified.

## Comparison

Repairing costs ¥110,000 less than replacing. At the estimated electricity savings of ¥1,500 per month, replacement would recover that difference in approximately 74 months, or just over six years, compared with repairing now. This calculation is only an estimate because the electricity savings are not guaranteed and the repaired refrigerator’s remaining life is unknown.

Replacement offers a new appliance and a five-year warranty, but it cannot be paid for from the current fund without reducing the money needed for school. Repair fits the current budget, but the refrigerator may require another repair or replacement sooner than expected.

## Proposed decision

Repair the refrigerator for ¥38,000, provided the repair can be scheduled without affecting the school payment. Keep the remaining appliance funds available for a future replacement.

Before approving the repair, confirm what the 90-day warranty covers and whether the quote includes all expected charges. If the refrigerator stops cooling, replacement takes priority and the family should revisit the budget immediately.
