# Family Discussion Memo: Refrigerator Repair or Replacement

## Current situation

- Refrigerator is 9 years old.
- It still cools, but sometimes makes a loud operating noise.
- Repair: ¥38,000, with a 90-day warranty on the repaired part.
- Replacement: ¥148,000, including delivery and removal, with a 5-year warranty.
- Estimated electricity savings from replacement: ¥1,500 per month. This is not guaranteed.
- Appliance savings fund: ¥220,000.
- School payment due next month from the same fund: ¥80,000.
- Borrowing and installment payments are not options.

## Budget comparison

| Option | Refrigerator cost | After school payment and purchase |
|---|---:|---:|
| Repair | ¥38,000 | ¥102,000 remaining |
| Replace | ¥148,000 | ¥8,000 short |

The replacement would require ¥148,000, but only ¥140,000 remains after reserving the ¥80,000 school payment. Therefore, replacement does not fit the current fund without finding an additional ¥8,000 from another source.

## Points to discuss

- Repair is immediately affordable, but the repair may only last briefly; its remaining life is unknown.
- Replacement provides a 5-year warranty and may reduce electricity costs by approximately ¥18,000 per year, but the savings are only an estimate.
- Compared with repairing now, the replacement costs ¥110,000 more. At estimated savings of ¥1,500 per month, that difference would take about 6.1 years to recover.
- If the refrigerator stops cooling, replacement takes priority, so keeping enough cash available is important.

## Proposed decision

Given the current budget, repairing the refrigerator is the only option that fits while preserving the school payment and leaving ¥102,000 in the appliance fund.

If the repair is chosen, the family should treat it as a temporary decision, monitor the noise and cooling performance, and continue rebuilding the replacement fund. If cooling stops, replacement should be reconsidered immediately, with the ¥8,000 funding gap addressed without borrowing or installments.
