# Family Memo: Repair or Replace the Refrigerator

## Current position

The refrigerator is nine years old. It still cools, but it sometimes makes a loud operating noise. We need to choose an option that fits the household budget while recognizing that its remaining life after a repair is unknown.

After setting aside next month’s ¥80,000 school payment, the appliance savings fund has ¥140,000 available.

## Options

| Option | Cost now | Warranty | Budget effect |
|---|---:|---|---|
| Repair | ¥38,000 | 90 days on the repaired part | ¥102,000 remains after the school payment is reserved |
| Replace | ¥148,000 | 5 years | Short by ¥8,000 after reserving the school payment |

A replacement would also be estimated to reduce electricity costs by ¥1,500 per month. This is only a store estimate, not a guaranteed saving.

The replacement costs ¥110,000 more upfront than the repair. At the estimated electricity saving, that difference would take about 74 months to recover—longer than the five-year warranty period. This comparison does not account for any future repair or replacement costs.

## What this means

At today’s prices, replacement does not fit the available appliance fund while keeping the school payment covered. Because the family will not borrow or use installments, it should not proceed unless another non-borrowed source of at least ¥8,000 is identified.

Repair fits the budget and leaves money available for an unexpected failure or another household need. Its main drawback is uncertainty: the refrigerator is already nine years old, the repair warranty is short, and repairing the noisy part does not establish how long the appliance will continue to work.

## Proposed decision

Because the refrigerator still cools and replacement is currently ¥8,000 beyond the available budget, proceed with the ¥38,000 repair if the noise is related to the quoted repair and the refrigerator is otherwise operating normally.

Keep the remaining ¥102,000 in the appliance fund after the school payment is reserved. Treat a complete loss of cooling as the trigger to prioritize replacement. If that happens, the family will need to identify a non-borrowed way to cover the remaining ¥8,000 or obtain another replacement price, since immediate replacement remains the priority.
