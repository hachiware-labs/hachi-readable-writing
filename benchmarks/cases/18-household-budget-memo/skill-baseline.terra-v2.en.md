# Refrigerator Decision Memo

The refrigerator still cools, but the intermittent loud noise may indicate a developing problem. Our decision should protect next month’s school payment while avoiding borrowing or installment payments.

The appliance fund has ¥220,000. After setting aside the ¥80,000 school payment, ¥140,000 remains available.

Repair costs ¥38,000 and includes a 90-day warranty on the repaired part. This would leave ¥102,000 in the fund after the school payment. The refrigerator’s remaining life after repair is unknown.

Replacement costs ¥148,000, including delivery and removal, with a five-year warranty. It is estimated—not guaranteed—to save ¥1,500 per month in electricity. However, replacing it now would leave ¥72,000 in the fund, creating an ¥8,000 shortfall for the school payment. Because we will not borrow or use installments, replacement is not currently affordable from this fund while preserving that payment.

The replacement costs ¥110,000 more than repair. At the estimated ¥1,500 monthly electricity savings, that difference would take about 73 months to recover, longer than the five-year warranty period. The replacement may still offer greater reliability, but we do not have lifespan estimates for either option.

**Recommended decision:** If the refrigerator continues cooling normally, choose the ¥38,000 repair and keep the remaining funds available. Reconsider replacement if the noise worsens, the repair does not hold, or additional funds are added after the school payment is secured.

If the refrigerator stops cooling, replacement takes priority. In that case, we need at least ¥8,000 beyond the current appliance fund allocation to cover both the replacement and the school payment without borrowing.
