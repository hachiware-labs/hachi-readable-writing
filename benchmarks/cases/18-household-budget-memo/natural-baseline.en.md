# Family Memo: Refrigerator Repair or Replacement

## Situation

Our nine-year-old refrigerator still cools, but it sometimes makes a loud operating noise. We need to choose between repairing it now or replacing it, without borrowing or using installments.

## Budget available

- Appliance savings fund: ¥220,000
- School payment due next month: ¥80,000
- Amount remaining after reserving the school payment: **¥140,000**

The school payment should be set aside before making the refrigerator decision.

## Options

|  | Repair | Replace |
|---|---:|---:|
| Immediate cost | ¥38,000 | ¥148,000 |
| Warranty | 90 days on repaired part | 5 years |
| Fund left after school payment | ¥102,000 | **¥8,000 short** |
| Future reliability | Unknown | Greater certainty during warranty |
| Estimated electricity savings | None stated | ¥1,500 per month, not guaranteed |

## Financial comparison

Replacement costs ¥110,000 more than repair. At the store’s estimated electricity saving of ¥1,500 per month, recovering that difference would take about **74 months, or just over six years**.

Over the five-year warranty period, estimated electricity savings would total ¥90,000. Even if the estimate proves accurate, replacement would still cost about ¥20,000 more than repair during those five years—unless the repaired refrigerator fails or needs additional work.

This comparison is incomplete because we do not know how long the current refrigerator would last after repair.

## Main risks

Repairing preserves more of the fund, but the refrigerator could fail soon afterward. If it did, replacement would take priority, yet only ¥102,000 would remain in the fund after the repair and school payment—¥46,000 less than the quoted replacement price.

Replacing provides a longer warranty and likely lower electricity costs, but the available fund after reserving the school payment is ¥8,000 short. Replacement is therefore not currently possible from this fund alone.

## Proposed decision

1. Reserve the ¥80,000 school payment immediately.
2. Confirm whether ¥8,000 can be paid from the current household budget without affecting essential expenses.
3. If that ¥8,000 is safely available, replacement is the lower-risk choice because the refrigerator is already nine years old, its post-repair life is unknown, and a failure would force replacement anyway.
4. If the additional ¥8,000 is not safely available, repair for ¥38,000 is the affordable immediate option—but we should treat it as temporary and begin rebuilding the replacement fund at once.

Before approving either option, we should also ask what fault the repair quote covers and whether the refrigerator is safe to continue using while it remains noisy.
