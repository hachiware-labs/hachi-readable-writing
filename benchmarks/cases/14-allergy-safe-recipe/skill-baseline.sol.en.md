# One-Skillet Chickpea and Vegetable Curry

A simple vegetarian curry for five, ready in about 30 minutes. Serve with the cooked rice provided separately.

## Ingredients

- 1 tablespoon vegetable oil
- 1 onion
- 2 carrots
- 1 red bell pepper
- 2 tablespoons curry powder
- 1 can chopped tomatoes
- 400 ml vegetable stock
- 2 cans chickpeas
- 150 g spinach
- Salt, to taste

## Before you begin

Because one family member has a severe peanut allergy, check the label of every packaged ingredient—including the curry powder, vegetable stock, canned tomatoes, chickpeas, and vegetable oil. Do not use anything that contains peanuts or carries a relevant precautionary label.

Wash the work surface, deep skillet, knife, cutting board, and utensils before cooking. Do not use utensils or oil that have previously come into contact with peanuts.

## Method

1. Peel and finely chop the onion. Peel the carrots, cut them in half lengthwise, and slice them thinly so they cook quickly. Remove the seeds from the bell pepper and cut it into small pieces. Drain and rinse the chickpeas.

2. Heat the vegetable oil in a deep skillet over medium heat. Add the onion, carrots, and bell pepper. Cook for 7–8 minutes, stirring regularly, until the onion is soft and the carrots are beginning to become tender. If the vegetables start to stick, add a small splash of the measured stock.

3. Add the curry powder and stir for 30 seconds, coating the vegetables. Keep it moving so it does not burn.

4. Pour in the chopped tomatoes and remaining vegetable stock, then add the chickpeas. Stir well and bring the mixture to a gentle boil.

5. Reduce the heat and simmer uncovered for 10–12 minutes, stirring occasionally. The carrots should become tender and the sauce should thicken slightly. If it becomes too thick before the carrots are cooked, add a little water.

6. Add the spinach a handful at a time, stirring until each handful wilts. Cook for another 2 minutes, then taste and add salt as needed.

Serve the curry hot with the cooked rice. This preparation reduces the identified peanut cross-contact risks, but the meal should not be described as absolutely safe.
