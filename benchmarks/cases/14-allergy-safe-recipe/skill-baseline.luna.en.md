# Chickpea and Vegetable Curry

Serves 5 · One deep skillet · About 30 minutes

## Before you start

For a severe peanut allergy, check the label of every packaged ingredient, including the chickpeas, chopped tomatoes, curry powder, and vegetable stock. Do not use any product containing peanuts or carrying a relevant precautionary label.

Wash the work surface, deep skillet, knife, cutting board, and utensils. Use utensils and vegetable oil that have not previously been used with peanuts. This meal is vegetarian and contains no meat, fish, dairy, or eggs, but no meal can be described as absolutely safe.

## Ingredients

- 1 tablespoon vegetable oil
- 1 onion, chopped
- 2 carrots, thinly sliced
- 1 red bell pepper, chopped
- 2 tablespoons curry powder
- 1 can chopped tomatoes
- 400 ml vegetable stock
- 2 cans chickpeas, drained and rinsed
- 150 g spinach
- Salt
- Cooked rice, to serve

## Method

1. Heat the oil in a deep skillet over medium heat. Add the onion, carrots, and bell pepper. Cook for 6–8 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the onion softens and the vegetables begin to soften.

2. Stir in the curry powder and cook for 30 seconds.

3. Add the chopped tomatoes, vegetable stock, and chickpeas. Stir well and bring the mixture to a steady simmer.

4. Reduce the heat to medium-low. Simmer uncovered for 10–12 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the carrots are tender and the curry has thickened slightly. If it becomes too dry before the carrots are tender, add a small amount of water.

5. Add the spinach and stir until it wilts, about 1–2 minutes. Season with salt to taste.

6. Serve the curry with the cooked rice.
