# One-Skillet Chickpea & Vegetable Curry

Makes 5 servings · Ready in about 30 minutes

Before starting, check the labels on the curry powder, vegetable stock, canned chickpeas, canned tomatoes, and any other packaged ingredient. Do not use an item if it contains peanuts or has a precautionary peanut warning relevant to your household. Clean the work surface, deep skillet, knife, cutting board, and utensils, and use clean oil and utensils that have not been used with peanuts. These precautions reduce risk but do not make a meal absolutely safe.

## Ingredients

- 1 tablespoon vegetable oil
- 1 onion
- 2 carrots
- 1 red bell pepper
- 2 tablespoons curry powder
- 2 cans chickpeas, drained and rinsed
- 1 can chopped tomatoes
- 400 ml vegetable stock
- 150 g spinach
- Salt, to taste
- Cooked rice, to serve

## Method

1. Dice the onion. Peel and slice the carrots into thin half-moons. Remove the pepper’s seeds and dice it. Drain and rinse the chickpeas.

2. Heat the oil in a deep skillet over medium heat. Add the onion, carrots, and pepper with a small pinch of salt. Cook for 6–8 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the onion softens and the vegetables begin to soften.

3. Add the curry powder. Stir for 30 seconds so it coats the vegetables and smells fragrant.

4. Add the chopped tomatoes, vegetable stock, and chickpeas. Stir well, scraping the bottom of the skillet.

5. Bring the curry to a gentle boil, then reduce the heat so it simmers steadily. Cook uncovered for 10–12 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the carrots are tender and the sauce has thickened slightly.

6. Add the spinach a handful at a time, stirring until wilted, about 1–2 minutes. Taste and add salt if needed.

Serve hot over the cooked rice.
