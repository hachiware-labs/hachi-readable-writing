## 30-minute chickpea and vegetable curry

Serves 5

This vegetarian curry contains no intentionally added meat, fish, dairy, or eggs. With a severe peanut allergy, however, do not describe it as guaranteed safe: packaging, manufacturing, and cross-contact risks must be checked every time.

### Ingredients

- 2 cans chickpeas
- 1 onion
- 2 carrots
- 1 red bell pepper
- 1 can chopped tomatoes
- 400 ml vegetable stock
- 150 g spinach
- 1 tablespoon vegetable oil
- 2 tablespoons curry powder
- Salt, to taste
- Cooked rice, for serving

### Before you start

Check the label of every packaged ingredient—including the chickpeas, tomatoes, spinach, vegetable oil, curry powder, and stock. Do not use anything that lists peanuts or carries a relevant precautionary warning such as “may contain peanuts.” If a label is missing, unclear, or the allergy information is uncertain, stop and replace the ingredient with a confirmed suitable product.

Wash your hands and thoroughly clean the work surface, deep skillet, knife, cutting board, spoon, can opener, and other utensils. Do not use shared utensils or oil that has previously contacted peanuts.

### Method

1. **Prepare the vegetables — 5 minutes.**  
   Peel and chop the onion. Peel the carrots and slice them thinly so they cook quickly. Remove the seeds from the pepper and chop it into small pieces. Drain and rinse the chickpeas.

2. **Sauté — 7 minutes.**  
   Heat the vegetable oil in a deep skillet over medium heat. Add the onion, carrots, and pepper. Cook for about 7 minutes, stirring frequently, until the onion softens. Keep the heat moderate so the vegetables do not burn.

3. **Add the curry powder — 1 minute.**  
   Sprinkle in the curry powder and stir continuously for 1 minute, coating the vegetables. If it begins sticking, add a small splash of the measured stock.

4. **Simmer — 12 minutes.**  
   Add the chopped tomatoes, remaining stock, and chickpeas. Stir well and bring to a gentle boil. Reduce the heat and simmer uncovered for 10–12 minutes, stirring occasionally. The carrots should become tender and the sauce should thicken slightly.

5. **Add the spinach — 2 minutes.**  
   Stir in the spinach a handful at a time. Cook for about 2 minutes, until wilted. Taste carefully and add salt as needed.

6. **Serve.**  
   Spoon the curry over the prepared rice, using clean serving utensils.
