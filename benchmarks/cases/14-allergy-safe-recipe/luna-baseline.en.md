## One-skillet chickpea and vegetable curry

Serves 5 · About 30 minutes · Vegetarian, vegan

### Before cooking

1. Wash the work surface, deep skillet, knife, cutting board, and utensils with hot, soapy water.
2. Use fresh oil and utensils that have not been used with peanuts.
3. Check the labels of every packaged ingredient: chickpeas, tomatoes, vegetable stock, curry powder, and vegetable oil. Do not use any product that lists peanuts or has a precautionary statement such as “may contain peanuts” or “processed in a facility with peanuts.” Such statements indicate possible cross-contact risk. [FDA](https://www.fda.gov/food/nutrition-food-labeling-and-critical-foods/food-allergies) [Food Allergy Research & Education](https://www.foodallergy.org/resources/avoiding-cross-contact)
4. Because the allergy is severe, this process reduces risk but cannot guarantee the meal is safe for that person.

### Ingredients

- 1 tablespoon vegetable oil
- 1 onion, diced
- 2 carrots, thinly sliced
- 1 red bell pepper, diced
- 2 tablespoons curry powder
- 1 can chopped tomatoes
- 400 ml vegetable stock
- 2 cans chickpeas, drained and rinsed
- 150 g spinach
- Salt, to taste
- Cooked rice, for serving

### Method

1. Heat the oil in a deep skillet over medium heat.
2. Add the onion, carrots, and bell pepper. Cook for 6–8 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the onion softens and the carrots begin to tenderize.
3. Stir in the curry powder and cook for 30 seconds.
4. Add the chopped tomatoes, vegetable stock, and chickpeas. Stir well and bring to a gentle boil.
5. Reduce the heat and simmer uncovered for 10–12 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the carrots are tender and the sauce has thickened slightly.
6. Stir in the spinach and cook for 2–3 minutes, until wilted.
7. Taste and add salt as needed. Serve over the cooked rice using clean serving utensils.
