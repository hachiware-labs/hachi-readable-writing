# One-Skillet Chickpea and Vegetable Curry

Serves 5 | Ready in about 30 minutes

Before starting, check the labels on every packaged ingredient—including the curry powder, vegetable stock, canned chickpeas, and tomatoes—to confirm that it contains no peanuts and has no relevant peanut precautionary label. Wash the work surface, deep skillet, knife, cutting board, and utensils, and use only oil and equipment that have not been used with peanuts.

## Ingredients

- 1 tablespoon vegetable oil
- 1 onion
- 2 carrots
- 1 red bell pepper
- 2 tablespoons curry powder
- 1 can chopped tomatoes
- 400 ml vegetable stock
- 2 cans chickpeas, drained and rinsed
- 150 g spinach
- Salt, to taste
- Cooked rice, to serve

## Method

1. Finely chop the onion. Cut the carrots into small, thin pieces and the pepper into bite-size pieces. Smaller pieces help everything cook within the time available.

2. Heat the oil in a deep skillet over medium heat. Add the onion, carrots, and pepper with a small pinch of salt. Cook for 6–8 minutes, stirring often, until the onion softens and the vegetables begin to lose their raw firmness.

3. Add the curry powder and stir for about 30 seconds, so it coats the vegetables and becomes fragrant.

4. Pour in the chopped tomatoes and vegetable stock, then add the drained chickpeas. Stir well and bring the curry to a gentle simmer.

5. Simmer uncovered for 10–12 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the carrots are tender and the sauce has thickened slightly. Taste and add salt if needed.

6. Add the spinach a handful at a time, stirring until it wilts into the curry, about 1–2 minutes.

Serve over the prepared rice. For a severe peanut allergy, serve only after confirming the ingredients and cooking equipment meet the household’s allergy precautions.
