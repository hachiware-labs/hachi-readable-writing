# Chickpea and Vegetable Curry

Serves 5 | Ready in about 30 minutes

This dairy-free, egg-free, meat-free curry is vegetarian and is served with cooked rice. Because one family member has a severe peanut allergy, check every packaged ingredient and keep the cooking area free from peanut cross-contact before you begin.

## Ingredients

- 1 tablespoon vegetable oil
- 1 onion
- 2 carrots
- 1 red bell pepper
- 2 tablespoons curry powder
- 1 can chopped tomatoes
- 400 ml vegetable stock
- 2 cans chickpeas
- 150 g spinach
- Salt, to taste
- Cooked rice, to serve

## Before you cook

Check the labels on the curry powder, vegetable stock, canned tomatoes, chickpeas, and vegetable oil. Do not use an ingredient if it contains peanuts or has a relevant peanut precautionary label.

Wash the work surface, deep skillet, knife, cutting board, and utensils. Use only clean utensils and oil that have not previously been used with peanuts.

Drain and rinse the chickpeas. Peel and dice the onion and carrots. Remove the seeds from the pepper and dice it. If the spinach is not pre-washed, wash it thoroughly.

## Method

1. Heat the oil in a deep skillet over medium heat. Add the onion, carrots, and red pepper. Cook for about 6–8 minutes, stirring often, until the onion has softened and the vegetables are beginning to soften.

2. Add the curry powder and stir for about 30 seconds, so it coats the vegetables and becomes fragrant.

3. Add the chopped tomatoes, vegetable stock, and chickpeas. Stir well, scraping the bottom of the skillet.

4. Bring the curry to a gentle simmer. Cook uncovered for 10–12 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the carrots are tender and the sauce has thickened slightly.

5. Add the spinach a handful at a time, stirring until it wilts into the curry. This should take 1–2 minutes.

6. Taste and add salt if needed. Serve hot with the cooked rice.

The meal is prepared to avoid peanut ingredients and cross-contact based on the checks above, but it should not be described as absolutely safe.
