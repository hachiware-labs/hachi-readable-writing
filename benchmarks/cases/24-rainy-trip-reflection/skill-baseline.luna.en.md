# The Kamakura Day We Didn’t Plan

On June 14, at 9:00 a.m., the three of us met at Kamakura Station for the first time in ten years. We had planned to walk along the coast and visit two temples, which sounded like a perfect way to spend a day together.

Then the heavy rain began almost as soon as we met.

One of us had new shoes, and before long, sore feet became another reason to slow down. We found a café near the station and decided to wait for the rain to ease. At first, we were all disappointed. We had made the trip to Kamakura, but we were sitting indoors, watching the morning disappear.

Gradually, though, the disappointment gave way to conversation. We began catching up on the parts of our lives that had not fit into our messages: changing jobs, caring for a parent, and a child’s schooling. Ten years had passed, and there was much more to say than we had realized.

We stayed in that café until after noon. The server refilled our hot water several times, quietly allowing us to remain at our table while the rain continued outside. The café was not part of our plan, but it became the place where we actually spent time together.

In the afternoon, the rain finally eased. We visited one nearby temple instead of two. The wet hydrangeas were beautiful, and the grounds were unusually quiet. We had missed the coast and much of the sightseeing we had imagined, but the temple felt peaceful in a way a carefully scheduled day might not have allowed.

On the way home, we agreed that next time we would not over-plan. More importantly, we promised to meet again within a year.

The day did not go according to plan, and I still regret missing some of the sights. But being stuck in that café gave us time to talk properly after ten years apart. Perhaps the most valuable part of the trip was the part none of us had planned.
