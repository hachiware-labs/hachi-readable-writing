# The Trip That Didn’t Go to Plan

When the three of us met at Kamakura Station at nine o’clock on June 14, it was our first outing together since university ten years ago. We had planned to walk along the coast and visit two temples. Then, almost as soon as we met, the rain began in earnest. One of us was already getting sore feet from a new pair of shoes, so we gave up on the morning route and took shelter in a café near the station.

At first, the disappointment was hard to ignore. We had all made the trip, and now the coast and temples we had come to see were disappearing from the day’s schedule. We kept watching the rain through the window, hoping it would ease soon.

But while we waited, we began to catch up properly. Our messages over the years had covered the outlines of our lives, but not everything underneath them. In the café, we talked about changing jobs, caring for a parent, and worries about a child’s schooling. The conversation moved easily between serious subjects and old memories, filling in the years we had known about only in fragments.

We stayed in the same café until after noon. The server refilled our hot water several times, and gradually the café stopped feeling like a place where we were waiting for the day to begin. It became the place where the most important part of the day was already happening.

The rain finally eased in the afternoon, and we managed to visit one nearby temple. We still missed the coastal walk and one of the temples we had planned to see, and that was genuinely disappointing. Yet the wet hydrangeas were beautiful, and the quiet grounds seemed especially peaceful after the long rain.

On the way home, we agreed that next time we would plan less—and that we would not wait another ten years. We promised to meet again within a year. Kamakura did not give us the day we had imagined, but the rain gave us time to hear what had been happening in one another’s lives. I would still like to return for the walk and the temple we missed. But I would not trade away those hours in the café to make the itinerary complete.
