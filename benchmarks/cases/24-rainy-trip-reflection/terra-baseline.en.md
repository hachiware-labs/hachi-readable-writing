## The Kamakura Day We Didn’t Take

We met at Kamakura Station at nine on the morning of June 14, three university friends together for the first time in ten years. We had a simple plan: walk along the coast, visit two temples, and make a proper day of it.

Then the rain began—heavy enough that the coast stopped feeling inviting almost immediately. One of us was also discovering that new shoes can turn a day out into a very long walk. After making the trip, it was hard not to feel disappointed. The day we had pictured was slipping away before it had really begun.

We found a café near the station and decided to wait for the rain to ease. At first, that felt like giving up on the plan. But once we sat down, the conversation gradually became less like catching up through messages and more like being friends again.

We talked about changing jobs, caring for a parent, and a child’s schooling—things too complicated or too personal to fit into a casual exchange online. The rain kept falling, and the hours passed. The server refilled our hot water several times, and before we knew it, we had stayed there until after noon.

When the rain finally eased in the afternoon, we went to one nearby temple instead of two. The grounds were quiet, and the hydrangeas were wet with rain. They were beautiful in a way they probably would not have been on the bright, busy day we had expected.

We missed the coast and most of our sightseeing plan, and it is still fair to say the trip did not go according to plan. But perhaps that was why it gave us something we had not planned for: enough unhurried time to hear what had changed in each other’s lives.

On the way home, we agreed that next time we would not over-plan it—and that we would meet again within a year.
