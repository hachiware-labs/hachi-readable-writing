### The Kamakura Trip That Didn’t Go to Plan

When the three of us met at Kamakura Station at nine on June 14, it was our first trip together in ten years. We had planned to walk along the coast and visit two temples, but almost as soon as we arrived, heavy rain began. Then a pair of new shoes started hurting one of us. After making the journey to Kamakura, it was hard not to feel disappointed.

We took shelter in a café near the station, intending to wait only until the rain eased. Instead, we stayed until after noon. As the server kindly refilled our hot water again and again, we began talking about things that had never really come up in our messages: changing jobs, caring for a parent, and worries about a child’s schooling. Ten years had passed, and although we had kept in touch, there was so much about one another’s lives that we had not known.

The rain finally softened in the afternoon, and we managed to visit one nearby temple. We missed the coastal walk and the second temple, but the hydrangeas, heavy with rain, were beautiful. The wet grounds were unusually quiet, and after our long conversation, that stillness felt especially welcome.

It was not the Kamakura trip we had planned, and I still wish we had seen more. But perhaps the bad weather gave us something the full itinerary would not have allowed: enough time to sit together and speak honestly. On the way home, we agreed that next time we would plan less—and, more importantly, that we would not wait another ten years. Let’s keep our promise to meet again within a year.
