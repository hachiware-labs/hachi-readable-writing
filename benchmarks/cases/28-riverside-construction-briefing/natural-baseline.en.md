# Resident Meeting Presentation Script  
**Riverside Walking Path Repairs — approximately 12 minutes**

## 1. Opening — 1 minute

Good evening, everyone. Thank you for coming.

Tonight, I will explain the upcoming repairs to the riverside walking path. The work will affect the approximately 600-meter section between Sakura Bridge and the Central Park entrance.

Construction is scheduled to begin on September 1 and continue through November 20, 2026.

We know that many residents use this path every day—for walking, cycling, exercising, taking children to nearby facilities, and getting around the neighborhood. Closing it will cause inconvenience, especially because the detour is longer. Tonight, I will explain why the repairs are necessary, how the closure will affect you, what safety measures will be in place, and where to find the latest information.

## 2. Why the repairs are needed — 1½ minutes

The city inspected this section of the path in July. Inspectors found 18 cracks in the pavement and four drainage problems.

The cracks can make the surface uneven and may become worse over time. They can present a particular risk to older residents, young children, wheelchair users, people pushing strollers, and cyclists.

The drainage problems also need attention. When rainwater does not drain properly, water may collect on the path. This can make the surface slippery and can cause additional damage to the pavement.

The purpose of this project is therefore to repair the existing cracks, improve drainage, and provide a safer, more reliable walking path.

We understand that a full closure is a significant disruption. However, the work involves both the pavement and the drainage system. For safety, residents cannot pass through the construction area while the work is underway.

## 3. Construction period and closure — 1½ minutes

The affected section will be closed at all times from September 1 through November 20.

To be clear, the closure will remain in place during evenings, weekends, and holidays, even when workers are not present. Please do not enter the closed section or move barriers. There may be open excavations, construction materials, uneven surfaces, or equipment inside the work area.

Work will normally stop on weekends and holidays. Construction vehicles will generally enter and leave the site on weekdays between 8:30 in the morning and 5:00 in the afternoon.

The project may be affected by weather or unexpected conditions beneath the path. If the schedule changes, the city will provide updated information. I will explain those communication methods shortly.

## 4. The detour and access to nearby facilities — 2 minutes

During the closure, pedestrians and cyclists will need to use the marked detour along a city street.

The detour is approximately 450 meters longer than the normal route. We recognize that this additional distance may be difficult for some residents, particularly older people, families with young children, and people with limited mobility. Please allow more time for your journey and, when possible, consider an alternative route or means of travel.

Cyclists must get off and walk their bicycles while using the detour. This is important because pedestrians, bicycles, and other street users will be sharing a more limited space. Walking bicycles will reduce the risk of collisions, especially in areas where visibility or space may be restricted.

Please follow the detour signs and do not try to take a shortcut through the construction site.

Central Park and the Riverside Children’s Center will remain open throughout the project. However, their entrances from the riverside walking path will be closed. Visitors will need to approach these facilities from their other entrances. Families going to the Children’s Center should plan a little extra travel time, particularly when accompanying small children or using a stroller.

## 5. Daily construction impacts — 1½ minutes

Most construction activity will take place on weekdays.

Residents may see construction vehicles entering and leaving between 8:30 a.m. and 5:00 p.m. Drivers and pedestrians near the site should remain alert and follow the directions of signs and safety personnel.

The noisiest part of the work will be pavement removal. This is planned for weekdays from September 7 through September 18, between 9:00 a.m. and 4:00 p.m.

During those dates and hours, nearby residents and users of the park or Children’s Center may hear machinery and pavement-breaking noise. The city has limited this work to daytime weekday hours to reduce the impact during early mornings, evenings, weekends, and holidays.

The dates may change if weather or site conditions delay the work. Any change will be announced through the city’s update channels.

## 6. Safety measures — 1½ minutes

Safety is a priority both inside the construction area and on the surrounding streets.

The work zone will be closed to the public at all times. Barriers and signs will show where the closure begins and direct people toward the detour.

The city has also considered the times when children travel to and from school. Large construction vehicles will not enter or leave the site from 8:00 to 9:00 in the morning or from 3:00 to 4:00 in the afternoon.

This restriction is intended to reduce interaction between large vehicles and children during the busiest school travel periods.

Even with this restriction, smaller vehicles or other activity may still be present near the site. We ask parents and guardians to remind children to follow the marked route, obey traffic signals, and avoid the construction entrances. All residents should pause and check their surroundings before passing a vehicle entrance.

Again, cyclists must walk their bicycles on the detour. We ask everyone to move carefully and give additional space to older residents, children, wheelchair users, and people with strollers.

## 7. How updates will be communicated — 1 minute

The current schedule is September 1 through November 20, but construction can sometimes be delayed by rain or unexpected site conditions.

If the schedule changes, updates will be provided in three ways:

First, information will be posted on the city website.

Second, updated signs will be placed at the construction site.

Third, registered residents will receive updates by email.

If you rely on email notices, please make sure that your resident registration information is current. We also encourage neighbors and family members to share important updates with anyone who may not regularly use the city website or email.

Before making a trip through the area, particularly later in the construction period, please check the signs or the city website for the latest information.

## 8. Summary and closing — 2 minutes

Before we take questions, I would like to repeat the main points.

Repairs will take place from September 1 through November 20, 2026. The approximately 600-meter section from Sakura Bridge to the Central Park entrance will be closed at all times.

The work is necessary because the July inspection found 18 pavement cracks and four drainage problems. Repairing them will address uneven surfaces, water collection, and further deterioration.

The detour follows a city street and adds approximately 450 meters to the normal route. Cyclists must get off and walk their bicycles while using it.

Construction vehicles will generally operate on weekdays between 8:30 a.m. and 5:00 p.m. Large vehicles will not enter or leave during school travel periods—from 8:00 to 9:00 a.m. and from 3:00 to 4:00 p.m.

Noisy pavement removal is planned for weekdays from September 7 through September 18, between 9:00 a.m. and 4:00 p.m.

Central Park and the Riverside Children’s Center will remain open, but their entrances from the walking path will be closed.

Schedule changes will be announced on the city website, on signs at the site, and by email to registered residents.

We apologize for the inconvenience and appreciate your patience and cooperation. These repairs are intended to make the riverside path safer and more dependable for everyone.

If you have questions after tonight’s meeting, please contact the Road Maintenance Division at **03-5555-2040**. The telephone line is open on weekdays from **8:30 a.m. to 5:00 p.m.**

Thank you. We will now take your questions.
