# Riverside Walking Path Repair Meeting — Speaker Script

## 1. Why we are meeting — about 1 minute

Good evening, and thank you for joining us.

Tonight I want to explain the planned repairs to the riverside walking path between Sakura Bridge and the Central Park entrance. This is a route many people use every day: older residents taking a walk, families going to the park or the Riverside Children’s Center, people commuting on foot, and cyclists.

The work will cause a temporary inconvenience, especially because this 600-meter section must be closed throughout construction. Our purpose tonight is to explain clearly why the repairs are needed, how the closure and detour will work, what we are doing to protect safety, and how you can receive updates if the schedule changes.

## 2. Why repairs are necessary — about 2 minutes

In July, the city inspected this section of the path. The inspection found 18 pavement cracks and four drainage problems.

Cracks may look small at first, but they can become trip hazards, especially for people using canes, walkers, strollers, or wheelchairs. Uneven pavement can also be dangerous for children running or for cyclists. Drainage problems are important as well. When water does not drain properly, puddles can make the surface slippery and can worsen damage to the path over time.

For those reasons, we will repair both the pavement and the drainage. The aim is not simply to improve appearance. It is to restore a safer, more reliable route for daily use and to prevent the problems from becoming larger and more disruptive later.

## 3. Construction period and closure — about 1½ minutes

Construction is scheduled from Tuesday, September 1, through Friday, November 20, 2026.

During that period, the affected section—from Sakura Bridge to the Central Park entrance—will be closed at all times. This full closure is necessary so that the repair crews can work safely and so that no one walks or rides through an active construction area.

Central Park and the Riverside Children’s Center will remain open during the work. However, their entrances from the walking path will be closed. Please use their entrances from the surrounding streets instead.

We understand that a continuous closure is a significant change. It allows the work to be carried out in a controlled way, with fewer risks for residents and workers than would be possible if the path remained partially open.

## 4. Detour route and what users should expect — about 2 minutes

The detour follows a city street around the closed section. It is approximately 450 meters longer than the normal route.

For many people, that will mean extra walking time. We especially ask families with young children, older residents, and anyone with limited mobility to allow additional time when traveling to Central Park, the Children’s Center, or destinations along the river.

Cyclists must walk their bicycles on the detour. This is an important safety rule. The detour uses a city street that will also be shared with pedestrians, including children and older adults. Walking bicycles helps reduce the risk of collisions and makes the route more predictable for everyone.

Signs will be posted at the closure points and along the detour. Please follow those signs rather than attempting to enter the closed path from another location. The work area may include uneven surfaces, equipment, and construction vehicles, even when workers are not immediately visible.

## 5. Daily construction activity and safety measures — about 2½ minutes

Construction vehicles will enter and leave on weekdays between 8:30 in the morning and 5:00 in the afternoon. Work will normally stop on weekends and holidays.

We have also set restrictions for the times when children are commonly traveling to and from school. Large vehicles will not enter or leave the site from 8:00 to 9:00 in the morning or from 3:00 to 4:00 in the afternoon.

This measure is intended to reduce vehicle movement during the busiest school travel times. Even so, we ask parents and caregivers to remind children to use the signed detour and to take extra care near the construction area and nearby streets.

The noisiest part of the project will be pavement removal. That work is planned for weekdays from September 7 through September 18, between 9:00 in the morning and 4:00 in the afternoon.

We recognize that this may affect people at home during the day, including older residents, families with young children, and people working from home. The noise period has been limited to daytime weekday hours, and it is scheduled for a defined two-week period rather than the full construction season.

The closure, signed detour, bicycle-walking rule, limits on large vehicles during school travel times, and the daytime schedule for noisy work are all part of the project’s safety approach. We ask everyone to help by respecting the barriers and signs.

## 6. How we will share updates — about 1½ minutes

Weather, site conditions, or other construction issues can sometimes affect a schedule. If there is a delay or an important change, the city will share it in three ways: on the city website, on signs at the site, and by email to registered residents.

If you would like email updates, please make sure you are registered to receive city notices. The signs at the site will remain important for anyone using the route day to day, because they can reflect the most immediate local information.

We will communicate changes as they become known. We do not want residents to arrive expecting one route or schedule and find something different without notice.

## 7. Closing and questions — about 1½ minutes

To close, the riverside path repairs will run from September 1 to November 20. The 600-meter section between Sakura Bridge and the Central Park entrance will be closed throughout that time, with a detour about 450 meters longer than the normal route.

The work is needed because the July inspection found 18 pavement cracks and four drainage problems. We are taking steps to limit risk and disruption, including restrictions on large construction vehicles during school travel times and a defined daytime period for the noisiest pavement removal work.

Central Park and the Riverside Children’s Center will remain open, although their walking-path entrances will be closed.

For questions, please contact the Road Maintenance Division at 03-5555-2040, weekdays from 8:30 a.m. to 5:00 p.m.

Thank you for your patience and cooperation while we complete these needed repairs.
