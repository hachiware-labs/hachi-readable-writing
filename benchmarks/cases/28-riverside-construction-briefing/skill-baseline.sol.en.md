# Riverside Walking Path Repairs  
## Resident Meeting Presentation Script

Good evening, and thank you for joining us.

Tonight, I will explain the upcoming repairs to the riverside walking path between Sakura Bridge and the Central Park entrance. I will cover why the work is needed, when the path will be closed, how to use the detour, what safety measures will be in place, and where to find updates.

The main point to remember is that this 600-meter section of the walking path will be completely closed from September 1 through November 20, 2026. During that period, everyone—including pedestrians, families with children, and cyclists—will need to use the city-street detour.

## Why the repairs are needed

The city inspected this section of the path in July. The inspection found 18 cracks in the pavement and four drainage problems.

Cracks make the surface uneven and can worsen over time. They are a particular concern for older residents, young children, people using wheelchairs or mobility aids, and anyone pushing a stroller. Drainage problems can also allow water to collect on the path, making the surface more difficult to use and contributing to further damage.

The work will therefore address both the visible pavement cracks and the underlying drainage problems. Completing these repairs now is intended to restore a safer, more reliable walking surface.

## Construction period and closure

Construction will begin on September 1 and is scheduled to finish on November 20, 2026.

The affected section runs for approximately 600 meters, from Sakura Bridge to the Central Park entrance. This entire section will be closed at all times during construction. The closure applies during the day, at night, and on weekends, even when workers are not present.

Please do not enter the closed section. A path that appears quiet may still contain unfinished pavement, open work areas, equipment, or other construction hazards.

Work will normally take place on weekdays. Construction vehicles may enter and leave the site between 8:30 in the morning and 5:00 in the afternoon. Work normally stops on weekends and public holidays, but the path will remain closed throughout the full construction period.

## The detour

The detour follows a city street and is approximately 450 meters longer than the normal riverside route.

Please allow additional time, especially if you walk slowly, use a mobility aid, travel with small children, or push a stroller. Follow the signs installed at the site rather than attempting to pass through the closed section.

Cyclists may also use the detour, but they must get off and walk their bicycles. This is important because pedestrians, children, older residents, and people with bicycles will be sharing the same detour space. Walking bicycles will reduce differences in speed and make the route safer for everyone.

If you are accompanying a child, please keep the child close while using the city-street portion of the route. We also ask all users to remain aware of ordinary street traffic and other people using the detour.

## Access to nearby facilities

Central Park and the Riverside Children’s Center will remain open during the repairs. However, their entrances from the riverside walking path will be closed.

This means that visitors will need to approach these facilities by another available entrance. Please plan your route before leaving home and allow for the additional distance caused by the path closure.

The facilities themselves are not closing. Only access from the construction section of the walking path will be unavailable.

## Construction traffic and school travel times

Construction vehicles will enter and leave the work area from 8:30 a.m. to 5:00 p.m. on weekdays.

The city has identified two periods when many children are traveling to and from school: 8:00 to 9:00 in the morning and 3:00 to 4:00 in the afternoon. Large construction vehicles will not enter or leave the site during those periods.

This restriction is intended to reduce interaction between large vehicles and children during the busiest school travel times. Other construction activity may continue, so residents should still take care near the work area and follow all posted restrictions.

Parents and caregivers should make sure that children understand that the riverside section is closed, even if it was previously part of their usual route. Please encourage them to use the marked detour and not to take shortcuts through the construction area.

## Noise during pavement removal

The noisiest stage will be the removal of damaged pavement.

This work is planned for weekdays from September 7 through September 18. During those dates, noisy work will take place between 9:00 a.m. and 4:00 p.m.

Residents near the path may hear construction noise during these hours. The work is limited to daytime on weekdays, but it may still affect people who are at home, families with young children, and people who work from home.

The dates may change if construction is delayed. If that happens, the city will communicate the revised schedule through the update channels explained shortly.

## What residents should expect each day

During the construction period, the most noticeable daily effects will be the full path closure, the longer city-street detour, construction vehicles on weekdays, and temporary noise.

The closure will remain in place outside working hours. Please do not assume the route is open because it is early in the morning, evening, weekend, or public holiday.

On ordinary weekdays, construction vehicles may enter or leave between 8:30 a.m. and 5:00 p.m., except that large vehicles will avoid the school travel periods from 8:00 to 9:00 a.m. and from 3:00 to 4:00 p.m.

From September 7 through 18, residents should also expect pavement-removal noise between 9:00 a.m. and 4:00 p.m.

These arrangements may require changes to familiar routines. We especially ask residents to consider the extra walking distance when planning trips to Central Park, the Riverside Children’s Center, or other destinations normally reached along the river.

## Schedule updates

The current construction period is September 1 through November 20. However, repair work can sometimes be affected by site conditions or other delays.

If the schedule changes, updates will be provided in three places:

- On the city website
- On signs at the construction site
- By email to residents who have registered to receive notices

Residents who rely on the path regularly should check one of these sources as the work progresses. Please pay particular attention to site signs, because they provide information where the route is affected.

Only registered residents will receive the email notices. Those who are not registered should use the city website or the signs at the site for updates.

## Closing request

To help the work proceed safely, please remember these five points.

First, the 600-meter section from Sakura Bridge to the Central Park entrance will be closed from September 1 through November 20.

Second, use the city-street detour and allow for approximately 450 meters of additional travel.

Third, cyclists must get off and walk their bicycles on the detour.

Fourth, Central Park and the Riverside Children’s Center will remain open, but their walking-path entrances will be closed.

Finally, check the city website, site signs, or registered-resident email notices for schedule changes.

We understand that this closure will inconvenience people who use the riverside path for daily walks, school travel, cycling, recreation, and access to nearby facilities. The repairs are needed because the July inspection identified 18 pavement cracks and four drainage problems. Addressing them requires a full temporary closure, but the result will be a repaired path with the identified pavement and drainage defects corrected.

For questions, please contact the Road Maintenance Division at **03-5555-2040**. Telephone inquiries are accepted on weekdays from **8:30 a.m. to 5:00 p.m.**

Thank you for your patience, for following the detour, and for helping keep the construction area safe.
