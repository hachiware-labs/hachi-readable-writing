# Riverside Walking Path Repairs: Resident Meeting Script

## Why we are meeting

Good evening, and thank you for coming.

Tonight, I will explain the planned repairs to the riverside walking path: why the work is necessary, how it will affect daily travel, the safety measures in place, and how we will keep residents informed.

The work will take place on the approximately 600-meter section between Sakura Bridge and the Central Park entrance. Construction is scheduled from September 1 through November 20, 2026.

Our goal is to repair the path before its condition creates a greater risk for people walking, pushing strollers, using mobility aids, or cycling through the area.

## Why repairs are needed

A city inspection in July found 18 pavement cracks and four drainage problems along this section.

The cracks can worsen over time and create uneven surfaces. The drainage problems can leave water on the path, particularly after rain, which makes walking less safe and can further damage the pavement.

The repairs will address both issues: damaged pavement will be repaired, and drainage will be improved so that water can move away from the path properly.

This is disruptive work, but completing it now is intended to provide a safer and more reliable riverside route afterward.

## What will be closed

For safety, the affected section will be closed at all times during construction. It will not be available for walking, cycling, or access through the work area.

The closure runs from Sakura Bridge to the Central Park entrance.

Central Park and the Riverside Children’s Center will remain open throughout the project. However, their entrances from the riverside walking path will be closed while construction is underway. Please use the entrances that connect from surrounding streets instead.

We know this changes familiar routines, especially for families walking to the Children’s Center, residents visiting the park, and people who use the riverside path for daily exercise or travel.

## The detour

The detour follows a city street and is about 450 meters longer than the normal route.

Signs will direct people from both ends of the closure. Please follow the signed route rather than trying to enter the closed section. The construction zone may include uneven ground, equipment, open work areas, and moving vehicles.

Cyclists must walk their bicycles on the detour. This is especially important because the detour is shared with pedestrians, including older residents, children, and people using strollers or mobility aids.

We ask everyone to allow extra time, particularly when traveling with children or when visiting Central Park or the Riverside Children’s Center.

## Construction hours and vehicle safety

Construction vehicles will enter and leave the area on weekdays from 8:30 a.m. to 5:00 p.m. Work normally stops on weekends and holidays.

To reduce risk during school travel times, large vehicles will not enter or leave from 8:00 to 9:00 in the morning or from 3:00 to 4:00 in the afternoon.

These limits are intended to reduce interaction between construction traffic and children traveling to and from school. Even outside those times, drivers and work crews will be operating near the site, so please use designated crossings and follow traffic guidance from staff and signs.

## Noise and the most disruptive period

The noisiest work—pavement removal—is planned for weekdays from September 7 through September 18, between 9:00 a.m. and 4:00 p.m.

During those two weeks, nearby residents may hear more construction noise than usual. We recognize that this can be difficult for people working from home, caring for young children, resting during the day, or simply enjoying the riverside area.

Pavement removal is scheduled during daytime weekday hours to avoid early-morning, evening, weekend, and holiday disturbance as much as possible.

Other construction activity will continue through November 20, but this September period is expected to be the most noticeable.

## What residents can do

The most important actions are straightforward:

- Use the signed street detour and plan for the additional 450 meters.
- Walk bicycles through the detour.
- Do not enter the closed path, even if work does not appear active at that moment.
- Use street-side entrances for Central Park and the Riverside Children’s Center.
- Give extra time for school, park visits, and other regular trips during the construction period.

For parents and caregivers, it may help to walk the detour once before the closure begins so that children are familiar with the route.

## Updates and questions

If the schedule changes because of weather or other construction delays, the city will announce the update in three places: on the city website, on signs at the site, and by email to registered residents.

For questions, please contact the Road Maintenance Division at 03-5555-2040, weekdays from 8:30 a.m. to 5:00 p.m.

## Closing

These repairs will temporarily close an important part of the riverside route, and we understand the inconvenience that will cause.

The work is being done because the path has cracks and drainage problems that need attention before they become more serious. By using the detour, observing the closure, and allowing extra travel time, residents can help keep the construction period safe for everyone.

Thank you for your patience and cooperation as we improve the riverside walking path.
