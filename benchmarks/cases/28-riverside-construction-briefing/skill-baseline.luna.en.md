# Riverside Walking Path Repairs  
## Resident Meeting Presentation Script

Good evening, and thank you for coming.

Tonight I will explain the planned repairs to the riverside walking path between Sakura Bridge and the Central Park entrance. I will cover why the work is needed, how the construction will affect daily travel, what safety measures will be in place, and where residents can find updates or ask questions.

The main point is simple: the affected section will be closed throughout construction, so everyone who normally uses this part of the riverside path will need to use the detour from September 1 through November 20, 2026.

## Why the repairs are needed

The city inspected the path in July. That inspection found 18 cracks in the pavement and four drainage problems along the affected section.

The repairs will address both conditions. The purpose is to restore the walking surface and improve the drainage systems so that the path can return to safe, dependable use. Because the work involves pavement and drainage, construction crews need access to the full section rather than working around people who are walking or cycling through it.

The affected section is approximately 600 meters long. It runs from Sakura Bridge to the entrance area of Central Park. Every part of this section will be closed during the construction period.

The planned construction period is September 1 through November 20. Work normally stops on weekends and holidays, but the path closure remains in place at all times during construction. Please do not enter the closed section even when workers are not present. Equipment, open work areas, uneven surfaces, or other construction conditions may remain.

## The daily impact

During construction, residents will notice construction vehicles entering and leaving the area on weekdays between 8:30 a.m. and 5:00 p.m. These vehicles will use the designated access route for the work.

To protect children and other pedestrians during school travel times, large vehicles will not enter or leave between 8:00 and 9:00 a.m., or between 3:00 and 4:00 p.m. These limits apply on weekdays.

The work normally stops on weekends and holidays. However, the closure and the detour continue, because the construction area must remain separated from the public until it is safe to reopen.

The noisiest part of the work will be pavement removal. This is planned for weekdays from September 7 through September 18, between 9:00 a.m. and 4:00 p.m.

Residents near the path may hear significant construction noise during those hours. The noisy work is limited to the scheduled weekday period, although other repair activities may continue during the broader construction period.

If you are planning a visit to Central Park or the Riverside Children’s Center, please allow extra time and follow the signs. Both facilities will remain open, but their entrances from the riverside walking path will be closed.

## The detour

The detour follows a city street and is approximately 450 meters longer than the normal route.

For older residents, families with young children, and anyone who needs more time or a steadier walking pace, this additional distance may be important when planning a trip. Please leave earlier than usual, especially for appointments, school-related travel, or activities with fixed start times.

The detour is a city-street route, so please stay on the marked path and follow traffic signals and other street signs. Take particular care at crossings and near vehicle entrances.

Cyclists may use the detour, but they must walk their bicycles. This rule helps keep the detour manageable for people on foot, particularly during busy periods and near crossings. Please dismount before entering the detour and continue walking beside your bicycle until you have left the detour route.

Parents and caregivers should explain the change to children before beginning the trip. Children may expect the familiar riverside path to be open, so please help them recognize the closure signs and stay with the marked detour.

Anyone using a mobility aid, pushing a stroller, or accompanying a person who needs additional time should plan for the longer route and allow space for other people using the detour. If the route feels difficult or crowded, please slow down and follow the direction of signs and staff at the site.

## Safety during construction

The most important safety measure is the full closure of the affected section. Please do not pass barriers, move signs, or enter the work area to save time.

Construction vehicles will be active on weekdays from 8:30 a.m. to 5:00 p.m., except that large vehicles will not enter or leave during the two school travel periods: 8:00 to 9:00 a.m. and 3:00 to 4:00 p.m.

These vehicle restrictions reduce vehicle movement during times when children and families are likely to be traveling, but they do not mean that the area is free of all construction activity. Please continue to use the detour and watch for signs, barriers, and vehicles.

When walking near the construction area, keep children close and do not stop beside entrances used by construction vehicles. If you are cycling, dismount before joining the detour. Everyone should use the marked route rather than creating an unofficial shortcut.

The city will adjust signs and guidance if conditions change. Please follow the latest directions at the site, even if they differ from what you remember from an earlier visit.

## Updates and schedule changes

The current construction period is September 1 through November 20, 2026. Construction schedules can change, so the city will announce delays through three channels.

Updates will be posted on the city website, on signs at the construction site, and by email to residents who are registered to receive city notices.

If you are affected by this route, please check one of these sources before traveling, particularly after severe weather or when you are planning a trip that depends on the path reopening on a specific date.

The site signs will be especially important for people already near the path. The website will provide the city’s posted information, and registered residents will receive announcements by email. Please rely on the latest notice rather than assuming that work has ended because a particular date has passed.

The Central Park and Riverside Children’s Center facilities will remain open, but their riverside walking-path entrances will be closed during the construction period. Please use the entrances identified by the facilities or by signs at the site.

## Questions and contact information

If you have questions about the repairs, the closure, the detour, construction hours, or schedule updates, please contact the Road Maintenance Division.

The telephone number is 03-5555-2040. The division can be reached on weekdays from 8:30 a.m. to 5:00 p.m.

When contacting the division, it may help to describe the part of the route or facility entrance you are asking about. If your question concerns a schedule change, please also check the city website and the signs at the site, because those sources will carry official delay announcements.

## Closing

To recap the key points:

The city will repair 18 pavement cracks and four drainage problems identified during the July inspection. The work will take place from September 1 through November 20, 2026, along the approximately 600-meter section between Sakura Bridge and the Central Park entrance.

That section will be closed at all times during construction. The detour follows a city street and is about 450 meters longer than the normal route. Cyclists must walk their bicycles on the detour.

On weekdays, construction vehicles may enter and leave between 8:30 a.m. and 5:00 p.m., except during the school travel periods from 8:00 to 9:00 a.m. and 3:00 to 4:00 p.m. Noisy pavement removal is planned on weekdays from September 7 through September 18, between 9:00 a.m. and 4:00 p.m.

Central Park and the Riverside Children’s Center will remain open, but their entrances from the riverside path will be closed. Schedule delays will be announced on the city website, at the site, and by email to registered residents.

Please use the detour, allow extra time, and follow all closure signs and safety directions. For questions, contact the Road Maintenance Division at 03-5555-2040 on weekdays from 8:30 a.m. to 5:00 p.m.

Thank you for your cooperation during the repairs.
