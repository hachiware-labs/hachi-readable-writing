Create a presentation script for a resident meeting about repairs to the riverside walking path.
The audience includes older residents and families with children, and the explanation should take about 12 minutes.

What we know:

- Repairs to cracks and drainage run from September 1 through November 20, 2026.
- The affected section is about 600 meters, from Sakura Bridge to the Central Park entrance.
- A July inspection found 18 pavement cracks and four drainage problems.
- The affected section will be closed at all times during construction.
- The detour follows a city street and is about 450 meters longer than the normal route.
- Cyclists must walk their bicycles on the detour.
- Construction vehicles enter and leave from 8:30 a.m. to 5:00 p.m. on weekdays. Work normally stops on weekends and holidays.
- Large vehicles will not enter or leave from 8:00 to 9:00 a.m. or 3:00 to 4:00 p.m. because those are school travel times.
- Noisy pavement removal is planned for weekdays from September 7 through 18, between 9:00 a.m. and 4:00 p.m.
- Central Park and the Riverside Children's Center remain open, but their walking-path entrances will be closed.
- Schedule delays will be announced on the city website, signs at the site, and email to registered residents.
- Questions go to the Road Maintenance Division at 03-5555-2040, weekdays from 8:30 a.m. to 5:00 p.m.
- The meeting needs to explain why the work is needed, daily impact, safety measures, the detour, and how updates will be communicated.
