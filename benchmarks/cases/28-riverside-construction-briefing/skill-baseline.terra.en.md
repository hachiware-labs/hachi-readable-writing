# Riverside Walking Path Repairs: Resident Meeting Script

## Why we are meeting

Good evening, and thank you for coming.

Tonight I will explain the planned repairs to the riverside walking path between Sakura Bridge and the Central Park entrance. This is a route used every day by older residents, families with children, walkers, runners, and cyclists. Closing it will be inconvenient, and we want everyone to understand why the work is necessary, how daily travel will change, and where to find reliable updates.

The repairs will run from September 1 through November 20, 2026. The affected section is about 600 meters long. During construction, that entire section will be closed at all times.

Our goal is to complete the work safely and restore a path that is more dependable for everyone who uses it.

## Why the repairs are needed

In July, the city inspected this section of the path. The inspection found 18 pavement cracks and four drainage problems.

Cracks may seem minor at first, but they can widen over time and create uneven surfaces. Those uneven areas are particularly difficult for people using canes, walkers, wheelchairs, strollers, or bicycles. Drainage problems can also leave water on the path after rain, making the surface slippery and allowing further damage to develop.

The work will repair the damaged pavement and improve drainage where problems were found. Doing these repairs now is intended to prevent the condition from becoming worse and to keep the path safer for daily use.

## What will be closed

From September 1 through November 20, the 600-meter section from Sakura Bridge to the Central Park entrance will be closed day and night.

The closure is necessary because the work involves pavement and drainage along the full section. Keeping the route open around active construction would create risks for pedestrians, workers, and people using bicycles or strollers.

Central Park and the Riverside Children’s Center will remain open throughout the project. However, their entrances from the riverside walking path will be closed while the path work is underway. Please plan to reach both facilities from their other entrances.

## The detour

The detour will follow a city street. It is approximately 450 meters longer than the usual route.

For many residents, especially those with limited mobility or young children, that additional distance may affect travel time and planning. We encourage everyone to allow extra time when going to the park, the children’s center, school, or other destinations along the river.

Cyclists must walk their bicycles on the detour. This is an important safety measure because the detour uses a city street shared with pedestrians and other road users. Walking bicycles helps protect children, older pedestrians, and everyone moving through narrower or busier areas.

Please follow the posted detour signs and use the designated route rather than trying to pass through the construction area. The signs will help residents, visitors, and emergency access remain clear and predictable.

## What to expect on weekdays

Construction vehicles will enter and leave the site on weekdays between 8:30 in the morning and 5:00 in the afternoon. Work normally stops on weekends and holidays.

We recognize that vehicle movement is a concern for families, schoolchildren, and nearby residents. For that reason, large vehicles will not enter or leave between 8:00 and 9:00 in the morning or between 3:00 and 4:00 in the afternoon. These periods have been set aside because they overlap with school travel times.

Even outside those hours, please take care near the work area and follow instructions from site personnel and posted signs. If you are walking with children, please keep them close when using the street detour or passing nearby construction access points.

## Planned noise

The noisiest phase is expected to be pavement removal. It is planned for weekdays from September 7 through September 18, between 9:00 in the morning and 4:00 in the afternoon.

We understand that this may be disruptive, particularly for residents who are at home during the day and for families with infants or young children. The scheduled time limits are intended to avoid early-morning and evening disturbance, but there will still be noticeable construction noise during those two weeks.

If the schedule changes, the city will announce the update through its usual project channels.

## Safety during construction

The central safety measure is the full closure of the affected path section. This keeps pedestrians and cyclists away from excavation, pavement removal, drainage work, and construction vehicles.

The detour and the requirement for cyclists to walk their bicycles are also part of that safety plan. They reduce the chance of conflicts between bicycles, pedestrians, children, and people who may need more time or space to travel safely.

We ask residents to help by respecting barriers, using the signed detour, and reminding children not to enter the closed path area. A short detour is frustrating, but it is safer than trying to move around active construction.

## How we will share updates

The planned completion date is November 20. Construction schedules can sometimes be affected by conditions at the site, so any delay will be announced in three ways: on the city website, on signs at the construction site, and by email to registered residents.

If you would like updates by email, please make sure you are registered to receive city notices. Site signs will be especially useful for people already traveling near the path, while the city website will provide the latest general information.

## Closing

To summarize the practical message for residents: the riverside path from Sakura Bridge to the Central Park entrance will be closed from September 1 through November 20. Please use the signed city-street detour, allow for an additional 450 meters of travel, and walk bicycles through the detour. Central Park and the Riverside Children’s Center will remain open, but their riverside-path entrances will not be available.

These repairs respond to the cracks and drainage problems found in July and are intended to restore a safer, more reliable walking path for the community.
