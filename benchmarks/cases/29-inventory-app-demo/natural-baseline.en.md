## 10-minute presentation script

### 1. Opening and purpose — 0:00–0:45

“Thank you for your time today.

We have created a prototype equipment-inventory app to explore whether we can make equipment management easier and more reliable for General Affairs.

Today, I’ll briefly explain the problem we are trying to solve, demonstrate the prototype, and propose a small one-month trial. At the end, we would like your approval to cooperate with the trial and to appoint one person to help prepare the equipment data.”

---

### 2. Current situation — 0:45–1:45

“Equipment is currently managed separately at four sites, with one Excel workbook for each site.

At the end of every month, General Affairs combines these files. This takes approximately six hours each month.

Because each workbook is maintained separately, item names are not always consistent, and some changes are not recorded promptly. In the most recent physical inventory count, there were 37 differences between the records and the equipment actually found.

The issue is not simply the time spent combining files. It is also difficult to know whether the latest location and condition of each item have been recorded.”

---

### 3. What the prototype does — 1:45–2:45

“The prototype puts equipment records from all sites into one shared inventory.

For each item, it stores:

- Asset number
- Item name
- Site
- Storage location
- Condition
- Last verification date

Users can filter the inventory by site or display only items that have not yet been verified.

A smartphone can scan the QR code containing the asset number. The user can then confirm the item’s condition, update its location, and record that the item has been verified.

The app also keeps a history of every change. The history shows who made the update, when it was made, and the values before and after the update.”

---

### 4. Live demonstration — 2:45–5:45

“Let me show you a typical inventory-checking workflow.”

#### Step 1: Show the unverified list

“I have selected the head-office site and filtered the list to show only items that have not yet been verified.

This gives the person conducting the inventory count a clear working list. They do not need to mark rows manually or compare several files.”

#### Step 2: Scan the QR code

“I’ll now use a smartphone to scan the QR code attached to this item.

The app finds the record using its asset number and displays the current item information.”

**Demo action:** Scan the prepared QR code and open the item.

#### Step 3: Update the storage location

“The recorded storage location is currently ‘Cabinet A.’ During the physical check, we found the item in ‘Cabinet B,’ so I’ll change the location.

I’ll also confirm the condition and save the verification.”

**Demo action:** Change the storage location from “Cabinet A” to “Cabinet B,” then save.

#### Step 4: Show the unverified list again

“Now I’ll return to the unverified list and refresh it.

The item has disappeared from the list because it has just been verified. This makes it easy to see what remains to be checked.”

**Demo action:** Return to or refresh the unverified list.

#### Step 5: Show the history

“If we open the item’s history, we can see who made the update, the time of the update, and that the storage location changed from ‘Cabinet A’ to ‘Cabinet B.’

This gives General Affairs a traceable record instead of relying only on the latest value in a spreadsheet.”

---

### 5. Expected benefits — 5:45–6:45

“This prototype is intended to test three potential benefits.

First, a single inventory should remove the monthly task of combining four separate workbooks.

Second, standard fields and one shared set of records should reduce inconsistent item names and missed updates.

Third, QR scanning and the unverified-item filter should make physical inventory counts faster and show progress more clearly.

The change history should also make discrepancies easier to investigate when they occur.”

---

### 6. Current limitations — 6:45–7:30

“This is still a prototype, so I want to be clear about its current scope.

It does not handle purchase requests or depreciation. It is also not integrated with the external accounting system.

The prototype currently contains only 30 sample records, and we have not yet tested its performance with a larger inventory.

For those reasons, we are not proposing a full rollout or replacement of the existing process at this stage.”

---

### 7. Proposed one-month trial — 7:30–8:45

“We propose a one-month trial covering approximately 500 items on the head-office floor.

This is large enough to test a realistic inventory workflow while keeping the scope manageable. The existing official records and processes would remain in place during the trial.

We will measure:

- The time required to prepare and register the equipment data
- The time required to conduct the inventory count
- The number of discrepancies found
- The number and types of questions raised by users

We will also confirm whether the app remains usable with around 500 records and identify any operational issues that are not visible in the 30-record prototype.

At the end of the month, we will summarize the results and use them to decide whether further development is justified.”

---

### 8. Requested decision — 8:45–9:30

“Today, we are asking General Affairs for two things.

First, approval to cooperate with the one-month head-office trial.

Second, appointment of one person who can prepare the equipment data with us. This person would help confirm the source data, standardize item names where necessary, and answer questions about locations and equipment status.

We will provide the data format and support the preparation work. We would like to agree on the trial schedule and responsibilities with the appointed person before starting.”

---

### 9. Closing and questions — 9:30–10:00

“To summarize, the prototype brings equipment information into one place, supports QR-based physical verification, identifies items that still need checking, and keeps a complete update history.

The proposed trial will let us measure the actual time savings and accuracy improvements before making any larger commitment.

May we have your approval to proceed with the one-month trial and appoint a General Affairs representative to help prepare the equipment data?

Thank you. I’m happy to answer any questions.”

## Demonstration preparation

Before presenting, prepare:

- A smartphone already connected to the prototype
- One printed and tested asset-number QR code
- An item initially shown in the head-office unverified list
- A known location change, such as “Cabinet A” to “Cabinet B”
- A history screen showing before-and-after values
- A backup recording or screenshots in case scanning or connectivity fails
