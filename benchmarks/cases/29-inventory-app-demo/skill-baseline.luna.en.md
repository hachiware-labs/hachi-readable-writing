# Equipment Inventory Prototype  
## Presentation Script for the General Affairs Team  
*Approx. 10 minutes, including live demonstration*

### 1. Why we are proposing this trial — 1 minute

Good morning, everyone.

Today I would like to introduce our prototype equipment-inventory app and ask for your cooperation in a one-month trial on the head-office floor.

At present, equipment at our four sites is managed in a separate Excel workbook for each site. At the end of every month, General Affairs combines those files. This takes approximately six hours each month.

The process also creates opportunities for inconsistency. Item names may differ between files, and updates can be missed. In the most recent physical inventory count, we found 37 differences between the records and the equipment that was actually present.

The prototype is intended to move part of the verification work closer to the equipment itself. We would like to test whether that makes inventory work faster, more consistent, and easier to trace.

### 2. What the prototype records — 1 minute

The app keeps the information needed to identify and verify each item:

- asset number;
- item name;
- site;
- storage location;
- condition; and
- last verification date.

Each update is linked to the person who made it and the time of the update. The history also shows the before-and-after values.

This means that when a location or condition changes, we can see both the current information and how that information changed. The purpose is not simply to replace Excel with another list. It is to make the verification process more direct and provide a clear record of updates.

### 3. How a verification works — 1 minute

During an inventory check, a user can use a smartphone to scan the QR code assigned to an asset number.

The app then displays the item’s information. The user can check the equipment, update its condition or storage location, and save the result.

Users can also filter the list by site or display only items that have not yet been verified. This gives General Affairs a practical way to see what remains to be checked, rather than relying only on separate files and manual comparison.

I will show this flow now.

### 5. What the trial will tell us — 2 minutes

The prototype is still an early version, so we are proposing a limited trial rather than a full rollout.

The trial would run for one month and cover approximately 500 items on the head-office floor. During that month, we would record four types of information:

- the time required to register equipment data;
- the time required to complete the inventory count;
- the number of discrepancies found; and
- the questions and difficulties raised by users.

These measurements will help us compare the prototype with the current process and identify what needs to change.

For example, a shorter count time would be useful, but it would not be enough by itself if discrepancies increased or users could not understand the workflow. We therefore want to look at time, accuracy, and usability together.

The trial also has clear limits. The prototype currently contains only 30 sample records, and performance testing has not yet been completed. It does not handle purchase requests or depreciation, and it does not integrate with the external accounting system.

Those functions are outside the purpose of this trial. The question for this phase is narrower: can the app support more reliable equipment verification on one floor?

### 6. Cooperation requested — 1 minute

To begin the trial, we are asking General Affairs to approve cooperation with the one-month test and appoint one person to prepare the equipment data.

That preparation would involve organizing the data for the approximately 500 head-office items and checking the fields needed by the prototype, including asset number, item name, site, storage location, condition, and last verification date.

The appointed person would also help coordinate questions during the trial and review the results with us at the end.

We are not asking for approval of a company-wide replacement today. We are asking for approval to test the workflow in a defined area, collect evidence, and decide the next step based on that evidence.

### 7. Closing — 1 minute

To recap the reason for this proposal: the current four-workbook process requires about six hours of monthly consolidation, and the last physical count found 37 differences between records and equipment.

The prototype lets users verify equipment at the point of inspection by scanning a QR code, updating location or condition, filtering unverified items, and reviewing a history of changes.

A one-month trial with approximately 500 head-office items will show whether this approach improves the process and what further work is required.

Today, we would like General Affairs to confirm two things: approval to cooperate with the trial, and the appointment of one person to prepare the equipment data.

Thank you.
