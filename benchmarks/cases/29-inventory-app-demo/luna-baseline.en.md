# Presentation Script: Equipment Inventory Prototype

**Total time: approximately 10 minutes, including live demonstration**

## 0:00–0:45 — Opening

“Thank you for your time today.

We would like to introduce a prototype equipment-inventory app and ask for General Affairs’ cooperation in a one-month trial.

The goal is simple: make equipment records easier to update at the point of use, and make inventory verification more visible and reliable.”

## 0:45–2:00 — Current situation

“Currently, equipment at our four sites is managed in a separate Excel workbook for each site.

At the end of every month, General Affairs combines those four files. This takes approximately six hours each month.

There is also a data-quality problem. Item names are not always entered consistently, and updates can be missed. In the most recent physical inventory count, we found 37 differences between the records and the equipment physically present.

The prototype is intended to address these update and verification issues. It is not intended to replace every equipment-related process.”

## 2:00–3:15 — What the prototype manages

“For each item, the prototype stores:

- Asset number
- Item name
- Site
- Storage location
- Condition
- Last verification date

Users can filter the list by site and can also display items that have not yet been verified.

Each update is recorded in the history. The history shows who made the update, when it was made, and the before-and-after values.

This gives us a clearer record of both the current status and the changes that led to it.”

## 3:15–4:15 — How the smartphone workflow works

“The main workflow is designed for a smartphone.

A user scans the QR code associated with the asset number. The item record opens, and the user can check or update its condition and storage location.

After the verification is saved, the last verification date is updated. The item then no longer appears in the list of items awaiting verification.

I will demonstrate that flow now.”

## 4:15–6:30 — Live demonstration

“First, I will open the list of items that have not yet been verified.

This item is currently shown in the list. Its storage location is recorded as [current location].

Now I will scan the QR code on the equipment.”

*Scan the QR code.*

“The app has identified the item by its asset number and opened its record.

I will change the storage location from [current location] to [new location]. I will also confirm the current condition.”

*Change the storage location and save.*

“The update has been saved.

The record now shows the new storage location and today’s verification date.

Let’s check the history.”

*Open the history.*

“We can see who made the update, when it was made, and the before-and-after storage locations.

Finally, I will return to the unverified list.”

*Return to the filtered list.*

“The item has disappeared from the unverified list because it has now been verified.

This is the key workflow we want to test: scan, confirm or update, save, and immediately reflect the verification status.”

## 6:30–7:30 — Proposed trial

“We propose a one-month trial involving approximately 500 items on the head-office floor.

This limited scope will let us test the workflow with real equipment and real users while keeping the trial manageable.

During the trial, we will record four measures:

- Registration time
- Inventory-count time
- Number of discrepancies
- Questions raised by users

These measurements will help us decide whether the prototype is useful and what needs to be improved before considering a wider rollout.”

## 7:30–8:30 — Scope and limitations

“To set expectations clearly, this prototype does not handle purchase requests, depreciation, or integration with the external accounting system.

It currently contains only 30 sample records, and performance testing has not yet been completed.

The proposed trial will therefore focus specifically on equipment registration, inventory verification, location and condition updates, and the clarity of the update history.

The trial is intended to evaluate the workflow—not to serve as the final production system.”

## 8:30–9:30 — Cooperation requested from General Affairs

“To begin the trial, we would like General Affairs to approve cooperation with the one-month trial.

We also ask General Affairs to appoint one person to prepare the equipment data for the approximately 500 head-office items.

That preparation would include confirming the asset numbers, item names, locations, conditions, and the initial verification information needed for registration.

We will use the trial results to report what worked, where users experienced difficulty, how many discrepancies were found, and what would be required for the next stage.”

## 9:30–10:00 — Closing

“To summarize:

The current process requires about six hours of monthly consolidation across four Excel workbooks, and the last inventory count found 37 record-to-physical differences.

The prototype provides a shared equipment list, QR-code scanning, visible verification status, and update history.

We are asking for approval to run a one-month trial with approximately 500 head-office items and for one General Affairs representative to prepare the initial data.

Thank you. We would be happy to take your questions and discuss the conditions for the trial.”
