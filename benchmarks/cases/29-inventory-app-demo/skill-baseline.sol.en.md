# Prototype Equipment-Inventory App  
## 10-Minute Presentation Script for General Affairs

### Slide 1 — Why We Built This Prototype  
*About 1 minute*

Today, I would like to introduce a prototype for managing our equipment inventory and ask for your cooperation with a one-month trial.

Equipment is currently managed in a separate Excel workbook at each of our four sites. At the end of every month, General Affairs combines those files—a task that takes about six hours. Because the workbooks are maintained separately, item names are not always consistent, and some updates are missed. In the most recent physical inventory count, this contributed to 37 differences between the records and the equipment we actually found.

The prototype is intended to test whether one shared inventory can reduce that consolidation work and make physical inventory updates more reliable.

### Slide 2 — One Shared Equipment Record  
*About 1 minute*

The prototype keeps the information needed to identify and verify each item in one place. For every item, it stores the asset number, item name, site, storage location, condition, and date of the most recent verification.

Users can filter the inventory by site. They can also display only items that have not yet been verified, which gives staff a clear working list during a physical count.

The prototype contains only 30 sample records today. It is not yet a production system; it is a focused test of the core inventory workflow.

### Slide 3 — Updating Equipment at the Point of Verification  
*About 1 minute*

A smartphone can scan the QR code containing an item’s asset number. The user can then confirm or change the item’s condition and storage location while standing beside the equipment.

This matters because an update can be recorded at the moment the item is checked. Staff do not need to write down a change and transfer it into a workbook later, when it may be forgotten or entered differently.

Every update is also recorded in the history. The history shows who made the change, when it was made, and the values before and after the update. This makes changes traceable without requiring General Affairs to compare multiple workbook versions.

### Live Demonstration — From Unverified to Verified  
*About 3 minutes*

I will now demonstrate the workflow we want to evaluate during the trial.

*[Open the unverified-items view.]*

This list shows the items that have not yet been verified. I will select an item by scanning its asset-number QR code with a smartphone.

*[Scan the QR code and open the item record.]*

The asset number identifies the record, and the current equipment information appears. For this demonstration, the item has moved to a different storage location.

*[Change the storage location and save the update.]*

The new location is now stored, and today’s verification is recorded.

Next, I will return to the list of unverified items.

*[Return to or refresh the unverified-items view.]*

The item has disappeared from the list because it has now been verified. During an inventory count, the remaining list therefore shows the work still to be completed.

Finally, I will open the item’s history.

*[Open the history view.]*

Here, we can see who made the update, when it occurred, and the storage location before and after the change. This gives General Affairs a record of the change as well as the current value.

### Slide 4 — What the Prototype Does Not Cover  
*About 1 minute*

The prototype is deliberately limited. It does not handle purchase requests, depreciation, or integration with the external accounting system.

It has also not undergone performance testing, and its current 30 sample records are not enough to show how it will behave with a larger inventory. For those reasons, we are not asking for approval to adopt the system company-wide. We are asking to test whether the inventory workflow is useful and practical at a controlled scale.

### Slide 5 — Proposed One-Month Trial  
*About 1½ minutes*

We propose a one-month trial covering approximately 500 items on the head-office floor.

During the trial, we will record four measures: the time required to register equipment, the time required to perform the inventory count, the number of discrepancies found, and the questions raised by users.

These measures will help us judge both efficiency and usability. Registration time will show the initial preparation effort. Inventory-count time can be compared with the current process. The discrepancy count will indicate whether scanning and immediate updates improve record accuracy. User questions will reveal unclear screens, missing guidance, and operational issues that the sample demonstration may not expose.

At the end of the month, we will review the results before considering any wider use or additional functions.

### Slide 6 — Request to General Affairs  
*About 1 minute*

To proceed, we are asking General Affairs for two things.

First, please approve cooperation with the one-month trial on the head-office floor. Second, please appoint one person to prepare the equipment data for the approximately 500 trial items.

The trial will not answer every question about a future inventory system. It will tell us whether a shared record, QR-code updates, an unverified-items list, and traceable history can reduce effort and improve accuracy in the work General Affairs already performs.

With your approval and a designated data-preparation contact, we can begin preparing the trial.
