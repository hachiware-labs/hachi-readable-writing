# Neighborhood Children’s Used-Book Exchange Activity Report

The neighborhood children’s used-book exchange was held at the community hall on July 12 from 10:00 a.m. to noon. Children brought books they had finished and received one exchange ticket for each book. Although 18 households registered in advance, attendance reached 27 households and 41 children, and 186 books were collected.

The larger-than-expected turnout caused congestion just after opening. Because there was only one check-in station and volunteers needed to inspect every incoming book, a line formed quickly. At 10:20 a.m., we added a second table and reassigned two floor volunteers to check-in. The line cleared at approximately 10:35 a.m. While the team adapted during the event, two check-in stations should have been available from the start.

Reference books and books for younger elementary students were especially popular. In contrast, many books intended for older children remained, so the available selection did not match demand evenly across age groups. Children who could not use all their tickets were told that the tickets would remain valid at the next exchange.

Many children began reading immediately after selecting a book. To give them a suitable place to sit, we added eight chairs along the wall. The event concluded without injuries or lost books.

At closing, 73 books remained. Only books whose owners consented to storage will be kept for the next event.

The next exchange is planned for autumn. Based on this event, we will prepare two check-in stations before opening and provide clearer advance guidance about the target age ranges for donated books. These changes should reduce congestion and help create a more balanced selection for children of different ages.
