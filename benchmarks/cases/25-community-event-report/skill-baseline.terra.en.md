# Neighborhood Children’s Used-Book Exchange: Activity Report

The used-book exchange was held at the community hall on July 12, from 10:00 a.m. to noon. Although 18 households registered in advance, attendance was higher than expected: 27 households and 41 children took part. In total, the event collected 186 books.

Children received one exchange ticket for each book they brought, which they could use to choose other books. Just after opening, check-in became congested. We had prepared only one check-in station, and staff needed to inspect each incoming book, so a line quickly formed. At 10:20, we added a second table and assigned two floor volunteers to help with check-in. The line cleared at about 10:35.

The exchange was especially popular for reference books and books aimed at younger elementary-school children. In contrast, many books for older children remained. This uneven selection meant that some children had fewer suitable choices than others, despite the overall number of books collected. Children who could not use all of their tickets were told that their unused tickets will remain valid at the next exchange.

Many children began reading soon after choosing a book. To make space for them, eight chairs were added along the wall during the event. There were no injuries and no reports of lost books.

At closing, 73 books remained. Only books for which the owners gave consent will be stored for the next event.

The next exchange is planned for autumn. To reduce waiting, we will prepare two check-in stations from the start. We will also provide advance guidance on the target age ranges of books that are especially needed, with the aim of creating a more balanced selection for children of different ages.
