# Neighborhood Children’s Used-Book Exchange Activity Report

The neighborhood children’s used-book exchange was held at the community hall on July 12, from 10:00 a.m. to noon. Children received one exchange ticket for each book they brought and used the tickets to choose other books.

Although 18 households registered in advance, attendance reached 27 households and 41 children. A total of 186 books were collected.

The higher-than-expected turnout caused congestion just after opening. We initially had only one check-in station, and volunteers needed to check every incoming book. At 10:20 a.m., we added a second table and assigned two floor volunteers to assist with check-in. The line cleared at approximately 10:35 a.m. For the next event, we plan to operate two check-in stations from the start.

Reference books and books for younger elementary school students were especially popular. In contrast, many books intended for older children remained. This uneven selection meant that some children could not find enough books they wanted and were unable to use all their tickets. They were informed that unused tickets will remain valid at the next exchange. Before the autumn event, we will provide clearer guidance about the target age ranges and types of books needed.

Many children began reading immediately after selecting their books. To give them a place to sit without obstructing the exchange area, eight chairs were added along the wall.

At closing, 73 books remained. Only books whose owners gave consent will be stored for the next event. There were no injuries or lost books.

The event attracted more families than expected and gave children an opportunity to exchange and enjoy books. However, the initial congestion and the imbalance in books for different age groups need improvement. The next exchange is planned for autumn, with two check-in stations ready at opening and better advance guidance to help provide a more balanced selection.
