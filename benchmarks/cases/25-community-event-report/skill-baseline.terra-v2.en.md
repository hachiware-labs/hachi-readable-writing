# Neighborhood Children’s Used-Book Exchange Activity Report

The used-book exchange was held at the community hall on July 12, from 10:00 a.m. to noon. Although 18 households registered in advance, attendance was higher than expected: 27 households and 41 children took part. Children brought books they had finished, receiving one exchange ticket for each book, and a total of 186 books were collected.

The opening period was congested. We began with one check-in station, and staff needed to check each incoming book before issuing tickets. This created a line immediately after 10:00. At 10:20, we added a second table and assigned two floor volunteers to help with check-in. The line cleared at about 10:35. The quick response helped, but the delay shows that one check-in station was not sufficient for the number of participants.

Children showed the strongest interest in reference books and books for younger elementary-school readers. In contrast, many books intended for older children remained. Some children did not use all of their tickets; they were told that unused tickets will remain valid at the next exchange. Once children had chosen books, many began reading them in the hall, so we added eight chairs along the wall.

At closing, 73 books remained. Only books whose owners agreed to storage will be kept for the next event. There were no injuries and no lost books.

The next exchange is planned for autumn. To reduce congestion, we will prepare two check-in stations from the start. We will also provide advance guidance on target age ranges so that the selection better matches the children who attend.
