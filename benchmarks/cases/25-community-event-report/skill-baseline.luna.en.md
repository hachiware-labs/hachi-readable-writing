# Neighborhood Children’s Used-Book Exchange Activity Report

The used-book exchange was held at the community hall on July 12, from 10:00 a.m. to noon. Although 18 households registered in advance, 27 households and 41 children attended.

Children brought books they had finished reading and received one exchange ticket for each book. We collected 186 books. Reference books and books for younger elementary students were especially popular, while many books for older children remained unselected.

A line formed shortly after opening because there was only one check-in station and every incoming book had to be checked. At 10:20, we added a second table and two floor volunteers to help with check-in. The line cleared at around 10:35. Many children began reading their new books immediately, so we added eight chairs along the wall.

At closing, 73 books remained. We will store only the books whose owners agreed to have them kept until the next event. Children who had unused tickets were told that their tickets would remain valid at the next exchange. There were no injuries or lost books.

For the autumn event, we plan to operate two check-in stations from the start and provide advance guidance about the target age ranges for donated books. These changes should reduce congestion and help create a more balanced selection for children of different ages.
