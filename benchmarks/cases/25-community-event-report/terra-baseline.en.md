# Neighborhood Children’s Used-Book Exchange Activity Report

The used-book exchange was held at the community hall on July 12, from 10:00 a.m. to noon. Although 18 households had registered in advance, 27 households and 41 children attended. Participants brought books they had finished reading and received one exchange ticket for each book. In total, 186 books were collected.

The event drew more participants than expected, but the opening period was congested. We initially had only one check-in station, and every incoming book needed to be checked before tickets could be issued. A line formed immediately after opening. At 10:20 a.m., we added a second table and assigned two floor volunteers to assist with check-in. The line cleared at approximately 10:35 a.m. This response reduced the delay, but the initial setup was not sufficient for the turnout.

Children most often selected reference books and books aimed at younger elementary school students. In contrast, many books for older children remained at the end of the event. This imbalance meant that the available selection did not meet all age groups equally well. Children who still had unused tickets were informed that their tickets will remain valid for the next exchange.

After choosing books, many children began reading in the hall. To make this more comfortable, eight chairs were added along the wall. No injuries or lost books were reported.

Seventy-three books remained at closing. Only books for which the owners gave consent will be stored for the next event.

The next exchange is planned for autumn. To improve the experience, we will prepare two check-in stations from the start and provide advance guidance on the target age ranges of books needed. These changes should reduce congestion at opening and help create a more balanced selection for children of different ages.
