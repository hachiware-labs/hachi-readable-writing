Write an activity report about the neighborhood children's used-book exchange.
Residents who could not attend should understand both what happened and what we need to improve next time.

What we know:

- It was held at the community hall on July 12 from 10:00 a.m. to noon.
- Children brought books they had finished and received one exchange ticket for each book.
- Eighteen households registered in advance, but 27 households and 41 children attended.
- We collected 186 books.
- Just after opening, a line formed because there was only one check-in station and each incoming book had to be checked.
- At 10:20 we added another table and two floor volunteers helped with check-in; the line cleared around 10:35.
- Reference books and books for younger elementary students were the most popular.
- Many books for older children remained.
- Children who could not use all their tickets were told the tickets would remain valid at the next event.
- Many children began reading after choosing a book, so we added eight chairs along the wall.
- Seventy-three books remained at closing. Only books whose owners consented will be stored until the next event.
- There were no injuries or lost books.
- The next event is planned for autumn. We want two check-in stations from the start and advance guidance about target age ranges.
- The report should be honest about congestion and the uneven book selection instead of simply promoting the event as a success.
