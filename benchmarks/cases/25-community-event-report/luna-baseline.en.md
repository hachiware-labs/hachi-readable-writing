# Neighborhood Children’s Used-Book Exchange: Activity Report

The used-book exchange was held at the community hall on July 12, from 10:00 a.m. to noon.

Although 18 households registered in advance, 27 households attended, bringing 41 children. In total, residents donated 186 books. Children received one exchange ticket for each book they brought and used their tickets to choose other books.

## What happened

A line formed shortly after opening because there was only one check-in station, and each incoming book had to be checked individually. At 10:20, we added a second table and two floor volunteers to assist with check-in. The line cleared at approximately 10:35.

Reference books and books for younger elementary students were especially popular. In contrast, many books for older children remained, showing that the selection was uneven across age groups.

Some children were unable to use all their tickets. They were told that their unused tickets would remain valid for the next exchange. Many children began reading immediately after choosing a book, so eight chairs were added along the wall.

At closing, 73 books remained. We will store only the books whose owners gave permission for them to be kept until the next event. There were no injuries or lost books.

## Improvements for the next event

The next exchange is planned for autumn. Based on this event, we will:

- Prepare two check-in stations from the beginning.
- Arrange sufficient volunteers to check books and guide participants.
- Provide advance guidance about the target age ranges and types of books needed, especially books for older children.
- Continue offering seating for children who want to read after choosing a book.
- Confirm storage consent for all remaining books before the event closes.

The event attracted more families and children than expected, but the initial congestion and uneven selection need to be addressed. These changes should make the next exchange smoother and provide a more balanced choice of books.
