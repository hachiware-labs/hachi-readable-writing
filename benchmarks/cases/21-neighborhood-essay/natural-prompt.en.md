Turn my thoughts six months after moving into a personal essay of roughly 900–1,200 words.
It will appear in a local community anthology.

What we know:

- I moved from Nagoya, where I had lived for 15 years, to Morioka in February.
- The move was for my spouse's job transfer, while I continued working remotely.
- At first I was afraid of walking on snowy roads, struggled to understand the local dialect, and rarely went out except to shop.
- I looked at Mount Iwate from the window every morning.
- In April, my neighbor Sasaki invited me to help tend the neighborhood flower beds.
- On the first day I did not know what to say, but while carrying soil people taught me about nearby shops and how to live through winter.
- I have joined them about twice a month since then.
- When I returned to Nagoya for a business trip in June, it felt familiar, but I also missed Morioka's quiet mornings.
- I now feel slightly disappointed when clouds hide Mount Iwate.
- I do not want to declare that my hometown has changed; the feeling is that I am gradually gaining another place where I belong.
