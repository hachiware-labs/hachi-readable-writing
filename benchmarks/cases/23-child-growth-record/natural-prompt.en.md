Write a family growth record about how our daughter learned to ride a bicycle.
Her grandparents will read it too.

What we know:

- She is seven and began practicing without training wheels in May.
- On the first day her father held the rear rack, and she stopped after half a lap of the park because she was frightened.
- Starting the next week, she decided to practice for only 20 minutes every Sunday morning.
- On difficult days she made the goal smaller, such as practicing only turns.
- In mid-June she fell and scraped her knee, then said she did not want to see the bicycle that day.
- The next week she did not ride; she only practiced using the brakes and getting off with her mother.
- On July 5, she rode about 30 meters along a straight path without realizing her father had let go.
- When she stopped and learned that he had released the bicycle, she looked surprised and then laughed.
- She still needs a little help starting and does not ride on crowded streets.
- We do not want to turn it into a simple story about never giving up. We want to remember that she rested when afraid and chose how to practice for herself.
