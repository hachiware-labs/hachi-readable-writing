# Learning to Ride

In May, at seven years old, she began practicing her bicycle without training wheels. On the first day, her father held the rear rack as she rode around the park. She made it only half a lap before fear became too strong, and she stopped.

Starting the following week, she decided that she would practice for just 20 minutes every Sunday morning. On difficult days, she made the goal even smaller. Sometimes she practiced only turning. This helped her keep going at a pace that felt manageable to her.

In mid-June, she fell and scraped her knee. That day, she said she did not want to see the bicycle. The next week, she did not ride at all. Instead, she practiced using the brakes and getting off safely with her mother. Resting and changing the practice were part of learning, too.

On July 5, she rode about 30 meters along a straight path. She did not realize that her father had let go of the bicycle. When she stopped and learned what had happened, she looked surprised—and then laughed.

She still needs a little help getting started, and she does not ride on crowded streets. But she has learned more than how to balance on two wheels. She has learned to notice when she is afraid, to rest when she needs to, and to choose a next step that feels possible. We are proud that she learned in her own way.
