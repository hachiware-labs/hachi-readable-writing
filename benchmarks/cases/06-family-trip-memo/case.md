# Case 06: Family trip memo

The document helps a family decide on a realistic short trip. It should turn constraints and preferences into a comfortable sequence, explain exclusions and backups, and remain easy for non-specialists to discuss.

The comparison should check whether the output reads as a recommendation rather than a database of attractions, whether mobility and weather shape the plan, and whether budget and timing remain credible without invented prices.

During the first baseline attempt, the English run searched external tourism sources and introduced cited facts that were not in the case. The prompts therefore explicitly prohibit external search so that later comparisons measure writing behavior against a stable fact set.
