## Recommended family plan

This uses all five candidates, but keeps each walking segment short and builds in seated breaks. The main adjustment is to treat Higashi Chaya as a brief visit and Kenrokuen as a shortened route.

### Saturday, August 8

| Time | Plan |
|---|---|
| 10:30 | Arrive at Kanazawa Station. Go to the hotel area and drop luggage. Confirm beforehand that the hotel will store bags before check-in; otherwise use station lockers. |
| 11:20–12:40 | Take a bus to **Omicho Market**. Eat seafood early, before the main lunch queues. Keep market browsing to one short section and sit for lunch. Shops generally operate around 9:00–16:00, but individual hours vary. [Official market information](https://visitkanazawa.jp/en/shops/detail_10030.html) |
| 12:40–12:55 | Bus to Higashi Chaya/Hashiba-cho. |
| 13:00–14:00 | **Gold-leaf workshop at Kinpakuya Sakuda.** The workshop is about 60 minutes, child-friendly, and the listed 13:00 session fits the arrival day. The bus stop is about a five-minute walk away. [Workshop details](https://visitkanazawa.jp/en/activities/detail_617.html) |
| 14:00–14:30 | Brief **Higashi Chaya District** visit: main street, photographs, and an optional café stop. Do not attempt a long neighborhood walk. |
| 14:30–15:00 | Bus back toward the station. |
| 15:00 onward | Hotel check-in, proper rest, then an early dinner near Kanazawa Station. Keep the evening free. |

For the workshop, five participants choosing the basic chopsticks option would currently be approximately ¥8,800 total; the operator says prices changed from June 1, 2026, so verify the final menu price. [Sakuda’s current price list](https://goldleaf-sakuda.jp/en/trial/)

### Sunday, August 9

| Time | Plan |
|---|---|
| 7:30–8:00 | Breakfast and water/restroom break. |
| 8:00–9:20 | Bus to **Kenrokuen**. Visit only the central highlights, using benches or a tea-house stop every 15–20 minutes. Exit through the same convenient entrance rather than crossing the whole garden. |
| 9:20–10:00 | Bus to the **21st Century Museum of Contemporary Art** and seated café/rest break before opening. |
| 10:00–11:30 | Museum visit. Choose only a few galleries; the building is barrier-free and has wheelchairs, restrooms, lockers, and a café. [Museum facilities and access](https://www.kanazawa21.jp/data_list.php?d=10&g=10&lng=e) |
| 11:30–12:30 | Lunch at the museum café or a nearby restaurant. |
| 12:30–13:30 | Bus back to the station/hotel area. Collect luggage and rest in the hotel lobby or station café. |
| 15:30–16:00 | Move to the platform, allowing plenty of time for the 16:30 train. |

Kenrokuen is open 7:00–18:00 in August. Admission is ¥320 for adults and ¥100 for children aged 6–17; people over 65 are admitted free with age identification. The garden has slopes, but wheelchairs are available at several entrances. [Kenrokuen official guidance](https://shiro-niwa.pref.ishikawa.lg.jp/kenrokuen-garden/e/info.html)

The museum’s 2026 exhibition **ROJŌ** runs through September 6. It is open 10:00–18:00 on Sunday; current listed prices are ¥1,200 for adults, ¥1,000 for people 65+, and ¥400 for students. [Exhibition details](https://www.kanazawa21.jp/data_list.php?d=254&g=126)

## Transport approach

Regular city-bus fares are currently ¥220 per adult and ¥110 per child per ride. [Kanazawa transport information](https://visitkanazawa.jp/en/getting-around-kanazawa)

For this itinerary, ordinary fares should be roughly ¥5,940 for the five people over six rides. Buy the ¥800 adult/¥400 child one-day pass only on a day when the family expects four or more bus rides. [One-day pass details](https://visitkanazawa.jp/en/feature/detail_539.html)

## Rainy-weather alternative

- Keep Omicho Market for a short seafood lunch, but skip extended browsing.
- Keep the gold-leaf workshop, since it is indoors; skip the Higashi Chaya stroll and return directly to the hotel.
- On Sunday, skip Kenrokuen if paths are wet. Go directly to the museum at 10:00, spend longer in its indoor public areas and café, then return to the station early.
- If rain is light, make Kenrokuen only a 15–20-minute visit near one entrance, rather than attempting the full garden.

Before booking, confirm the hotel’s luggage storage, reserve the 13:00 Sakuda workshop as soon as possible, and recheck museum tickets, workshop prices, and any temporary closures. Sakuda notes that popular sessions can fill and online reservations generally close by 16:00 the previous day.
