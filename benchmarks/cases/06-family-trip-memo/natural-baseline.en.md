## Recommended family plan

This keeps the trip to four main experiences: Omicho Market, a seated gold-leaf workshop, a brief look at Higashi Chaya, and a carefully shortened Kenrokuen visit. The museum is a low-pressure optional stop and the main rain substitute.

I have interpreted “no car” as no rental/private car, while allowing taxis. That is the easiest way to protect the grandfather’s walking limit. A bus-only alternative is below.

### Saturday, August 8

| Time | Plan | Mobility notes |
|---|---|---|
| 10:30 | Arrive at Kanazawa Station | Use station toilets, then leave luggage at the hotel. |
| 10:55 | Taxi to Omicho Market | Reserve a five-seat taxi/van or split into two regular taxis. |
| 11:10–12:05 | Reserved seafood lunch | Reserve a table; do not join a walk-in queue. |
| 12:05–12:25 | One short market circuit | Pick one aisle or a few stalls, then sit. Omicho’s general hours are 9:00–16:00, although individual shops vary. [Official market information](https://visitkanazawa.jp/en/spot/detail_10030.html) |
| 12:25–12:40 | Seated drink/rest | Use this as a firm recovery stop. |
| 12:40 | Taxi to Kinpakuya Sakuda | Avoid walking from Omicho to Higashi. |
| 13:00–14:00 | Gold-leaf workshop | A useful one-hour seated activity. |
| 14:00–14:20 | Very short Higashi Chaya look | See the main street and one side street only. If crowded, take a photograph and leave rather than pushing through. |
| 14:25 | Taxi to hotel | Check in shortly after 15:00. |
| 15:00–17:30 | Hotel rest | No scheduled sightseeing. |
| 18:00–19:15 | Reserved dinner near the station | Choose somewhere within the hotel or station complex. |

Kinpakuya Sakuda currently offers sessions at 9:00, 10:30, 13:00 and 15:00, lasting about 60 minutes. Prices start at ¥1,760, and the activity is suitable for children and adults. Online reservations ordinarily close at 16:00 the previous day. [Official workshop details](https://visitkanazawa.jp/en/activities/detail_617.html)

The live booking page did not reliably expose August 8 availability during this check, so the 13:00 session is **not yet confirmed**. Reserve it promptly or telephone `076-251-6777`. Selecting the ¥1,760 chopsticks or pencils keeps the budget comfortable; larger items cost up to about ¥2,970. [Workshop price list](https://goldleaf-sakuda.jp/trial/)

Kenrokuen is open 7:00–18:00 in August. Admission is ¥320 per adult and ¥100 for ages 6–17; visitors over 65 enter free upon showing passports. Wheelchairs are free at several entrances, although the garden has slopes. [Official Kenrokuen guidance](https://shiro-niwa.pref.ishikawa.lg.jp/kenrokuen-garden/e/info.html)

On August 9, the museum’s exhibition zone is scheduled to be open 10:00–18:00. The current *Walking, Staying* collection exhibition runs through October 18; web tickets are ¥360 for adults, ¥360 for those 65+, and free for students. [Museum hours](https://www.kanazawa21.jp/data_list.php?d=5&g=7) and [exhibition details](https://www.kanazawa21.jp/data_list.php?d=256&g=126)

I would skip the underground *Swimming Pool*. It requires an exhibition ticket plus a same-day waiting-list number, can fill up, and weekend ticket counters can be crowded—the opposite of this trip’s priorities. [Official reservation rules](https://www.kanazawa21.jp/data_list.php?d=1&g=120&lng=e)

## Rain plan

Saturday already works well in rain because the market and workshop are indoors. Skip the Higashi Chaya walk and return to the hotel immediately after the workshop.

On Sunday:

- Leave the hotel around 9:30 and omit Kenrokuen.
- Visit the museum from 10:00–11:30, with frequent sitting breaks.
- Use advance web tickets for the collection exhibition.
- Return to the station area for an early lunch and a relaxed afternoon.

The paid *ROJŌ* exhibition is another rain option, running through September 6. Admission is ¥1,000 with web tickets for adults and ¥1,000 for those 65+, but the cheaper collection exhibition is probably enough for this group. [Current exhibition information](https://www.kanazawa21.jp/data_list.php?d=254&g=126)

The taxi amount is a planning allowance, not a quoted fare. Because five passengers may not fit in one ordinary taxi, ask the hotel to reserve a five-seat van or Universal Design taxi and confirm the expected charges. If two taxis are needed for every transfer, retain the entire ¥13,000 allocation.

If taxis are not allowed at all, local and Loop buses serve these areas. A one-day pass currently costs ¥800 per adult and ¥400 per child, or ¥3,600 for this family per day. [Official bus-pass information](https://visitkanazawa.jp/en/faq/) Buses will be cheaper, but may involve standing, waiting and crowding, so I would favor taxis for the garden and Higashi transfers.
