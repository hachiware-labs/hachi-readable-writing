# Kanazawa Family Trip Plan  
**Saturday–Sunday, August 8–9, 2026**

This plan favors short outings, seated breaks, and flexible timing. It includes the family’s main interests without trying to visit every candidate.

## Proposed itinerary

### Day 1: Omicho Market and a gentle afternoon

**10:30 a.m. — Arrive at Kanazawa Station**

Leave luggage at the hotel or in station lockers. Allow time for restrooms and a seated drink before continuing.

**11:15 a.m.–1:00 p.m. — Omicho Market and lunch**

Travel by bus or taxi. Walk through only part of the market, then have an early seafood lunch. If restaurants have long lines, choose a quieter nearby restaurant rather than waiting.

**1:00–2:00 p.m. — Rest near the market or station**

Take a café break, then return to the hotel area. Using a taxi may be worthwhile if it is hot, raining, or anyone is tired.

**3:00–4:30 p.m. — Hotel check-in and full rest**

This is the main recovery period of the day.

**5:00–6:00 p.m. — Optional short outing near Kanazawa Station**

Visit the station concourse or nearby shops. The grandparents can remain at the hotel if they prefer.

**6:00 p.m. — Dinner near the hotel**

Reserve a restaurant with seating and choose somewhere within a short walk or taxi ride.

### Day 2: Kenrokuen and a gold-leaf workshop

**8:00 a.m. — Breakfast and check-out preparation**

Ask the hotel to hold the luggage after check-out.

**9:00–10:30 a.m. — Kenrokuen**

Travel by taxi to reduce standing and walking. Choose a short route rather than attempting the entire garden. Stop at a teahouse or bench after no more than 15–20 minutes of walking.

Summer heat may be more tiring than the distance itself, so carry water and leave early if needed.

**10:30–11:15 a.m. — Seated break**

Have a drink or snack near the garden before the next activity.

**11:30 a.m.–12:30 p.m. — Gold-leaf workshop**

A seated craft activity should suit both the child and grandparents. Select a venue with a simple project that can be completed in about an hour.

**12:45–2:00 p.m. — Lunch**

Choose a reserved restaurant near the workshop or take a taxi back toward Kanazawa Station for lunch.

**2:00–3:15 p.m. — Hotel or station break**

Collect the luggage and spend the remaining time in a café or the station shopping area. This buffer protects against delays and gives everyone time to rest.

**3:30 p.m. — Be at Kanazawa Station**

Buy drinks or snacks and use the restroom before boarding.

**4:30 p.m. — Depart**

## Rainy-weather alternative

If steady rain is expected, replace Kenrokuen with the **21st Century Museum of Contemporary Art**, provided suitable exhibitions are open and timed admission is available. Use a taxi between the hotel, museum, workshop, and station.

Keep the museum visit to about 60–90 minutes. Large museums can involve considerable standing, so identify seating on arrival and take a break after each gallery area. If admission is unavailable or the museum is crowded, skip it and allow more time for the gold-leaf workshop, lunch, and station shopping.

Omicho Market is partly covered, but travel to and within the market may still be uncomfortable in heavy rain. On a wet first day, have lunch near the station and move the market visit to Sunday only if conditions improve.

## Activities to leave out

Higashi Chaya District is not included in the main plan. Its attractive streets fit the family’s interests, but adding it would increase walking and make the weekend feel rushed. It could replace the optional station outing on Saturday only if everyone feels rested; travel there and back by taxi and limit the visit to one street or shop.

## Points to confirm before booking

- Opening hours and any August closures for Kenrokuen, the museum, Omicho Market, and workshop venues  
- Gold-leaf workshop availability for five people, including a six-year-old  
- Museum exhibition access and whether timed tickets are required  
- Hotel luggage storage before check-in and after check-out  
- Restaurant reservations, seating, and child-friendly menu options  
- Workshop and restaurant accessibility, including stairs and nearby drop-off points  
- Cancellation terms in case weather changes the itinerary  

The family’s main decision is whether to prioritize **Kenrokuen in fair weather** or choose the **museum for a more weather-resistant and less exposed second morning**. The gold-leaf workshop remains the most suitable shared activity in either version.
