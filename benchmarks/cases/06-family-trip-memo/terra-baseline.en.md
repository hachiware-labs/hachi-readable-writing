# Kanazawa family trip plan — Sat–Sun, August 8–9, 2026

This plan keeps each outdoor walking stretch to about 15–20 minutes, uses seated activities as built-in breaks, and leaves generous time at the hotel/station. The key choices are: seafood at Omicho before the crowds build further, one pre-booked gold-leaf workshop, and a short garden visit using a loan wheelchair if helpful.

## Saturday: market, gold-leaf craft, and an early evening

| Time | Plan | Pace notes |
|---|---|---|
| 10:30 | Arrive at Kanazawa Station; leave bags at the hotel | Confirm in advance that the hotel can store bags before check-in. |
| 10:50–12:00 | Bus to **Omicho Market**; reserved seated seafood lunch | Ask the restaurant for a table reservation. Limit browsing to 15–20 minutes before or after lunch; the market’s standard hours are 9:00–16:00, with individual shops varying. [Omicho Market](https://visitkanazawa.jp/en/shops/detail_10030.html) |
| 12:00–12:25 | Brief market browsing / sit with drinks | The grandparents can stay seated while the parents and child take turns browsing. |
| 12:25–12:50 | Kanazawa Loop Bus to Higashi Chaya | The clockwise/right loop links Kanazawa Station, Omicho, Higashi Chaya, Kenrokuen, and the 21st Century Museum; it normally runs every 15 minutes. [Bus routes and fares](https://visitkanazawa.jp/en/getting-around-kanazawa) |
| 13:00–14:00 | **Gold-leaf workshop at Gold Leaf Sakuda** | Reserve the 13:00 session for all five. It is about one hour and starts from ¥1,760 per person. Confirm step-free access, chairs with backs, and the child’s participation when booking. [Workshop details](https://visitkanazawa.jp/en/activities/detail_617.html) |
| 14:00–14:25 | Very short Higashi Chaya look-around | Stay on the main street only; do not try to “cover” the district. If anyone is tired, go straight to the hotel. |
| 15:00–17:15 | Check in and rest | A real break here is deliberate, not spare time. |
| 18:00–19:30 | Reserved seafood dinner near Kanazawa Station | Choose a restaurant within a few minutes of the hotel, with table seating and a child menu if needed. |
| After dinner | Return to hotel | Keep the evening optional and local. |

## Sunday: garden, lunch, a short museum visit, then the train

| Time | Plan | Pace notes |
|---|---|---|
| 9:15 | Check out; leave bags at the hotel | Keep passports handy: they are useful for the grandparents’ Kenrokuen admission exemption. |
| 9:35–9:55 | Short taxi to Kenrokuen’s **Kodatsuno Entrance** | This avoids a longer uphill approach. It is reasonable to use taxis for these short, fatigue-saving hops even without a car. |
| 10:00–11:20 | **Kenrokuen Garden** | Borrow a free wheelchair at the entrance if the grandfather would benefit from it, even for only part of the visit. The garden has slopes; wheelchairs are available at Kodatsuno and several other entrances. Adult admission is ¥320, child admission ¥100, and visitors over 65 are exempt with a passport. [Kenrokuen visitor guidance](https://shiro-niwa.pref.ishikawa.lg.jp/kenrokuen-garden/e/info.html) |
| 11:30–12:35 | Reserved seated lunch near Kenrokuen / Hirosaka | Seafood or Kaga cuisine; prioritize a quiet table over a famous walk-in spot. |
| 12:45–14:00 | **21st Century Museum of Contemporary Art** | Focus on the Collection Exhibition rather than trying to see everything. The museum is barrier-free and has a café. The 2026 Collection Exhibition runs through October 18; for this family it is ¥1,620 total (two adults, two seniors, and one elementary-school child). [Museum tickets and exhibitions](https://www.kanazawa21.jp/data_list.php?d=1&g=7) |
| 14:00–14:25 | Taxi or bus back to Kanazawa Station | Collect bags, then settle into the station. |
| 14:30–16:05 | Snacks, souvenirs, toilets, and a seated wait | Do not add another attraction here. Aim to be at the platform around 16:05. |
| 16:30 | Depart Kanazawa | |

The museum is open on both proposed dates; its exhibition zone is generally open 10:00–18:00 and is barrier-free. [Visitor information](https://www.kanazawa21.jp/data_list.php?d=5&g=7)

## Rainy-weather alternative

Keep Saturday’s workshop, but skip the open-ended Higashi Chaya stroll and take a taxi from the market to the workshop and then to the hotel.

On Sunday, replace Kenrokuen with a longer, unhurried museum morning:

- 10:00–11:45: 21st Century Museum, including the Collection Exhibition and café break.
- 12:00–13:00: reserved lunch nearby.
- 13:20: return to Kanazawa Station/hotel area.
- 13:45–16:05: relaxed station shopping, snacks, and seated waiting.

The museum’s special exhibition is also running that weekend, but it adds ¥4,800 for this family and may require a timed online ticket. Treat it as an optional upgrade, not part of the base plan. [Online ticket information](https://www.kanazawa21.jp/ticket/)

## Decisions to make before booking

- Reserve a seafood lunch at Omicho and dinner near the station.
- Book the 13:00 gold-leaf workshop; explicitly ask about step-free access, seating, and cancellation terms.
- Confirm the hotel will store luggage before check-in and after checkout.
- Decide whether the grandfather would prefer to use Kenrokuen’s free wheelchair from the start.
- Check the forecast about a week ahead; if rain or extreme heat is likely, adopt the museum-first Sunday plan.
