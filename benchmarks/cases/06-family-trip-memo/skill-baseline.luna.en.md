# Kanazawa Family Trip Plan

**Dates:** Saturday–Sunday, August 8–9, 2026  
**Travelers:** Two grandparents, two parents, and one six-year-old child  
**Base:** Hotel near Kanazawa Station  
**Main priority:** Enjoy gardens, crafts, seafood, and indoor attractions without making the grandfather walk continuously for more than 20 minutes.

The main plan includes Omicho Market, the 21st Century Museum, Kenrokuen, and a gold-leaf workshop. Higashi Chaya District is left out because adding it would increase walking, waiting, and weekend crowd exposure.

## Why Higashi Chaya District is not in the main plan

Higashi Chaya is attractive, but the official guide estimates about 60 minutes for the district itself and about 20 minutes on foot from Kenrokuen. Weekend queues at popular venues can also reach one to two hours. [Official district information](https://visitkanazawa.jp/en/spot/detail_10212.html), [official weekend-crowd guidance](https://visitkanazawa.jp/en/feature/detail_210.html)

If the family strongly wants to visit it, use it as a replacement for the museum or Kenrokuen—not as an additional stop. Keep the visit to the main street and a rest stop, and travel by bus.

## Rainy-weather alternative

### Light rain

Keep the main plan, but:

- Use buses between every area.
- Shorten Kenrokuen to 15–20 minutes or skip it if the ground is slippery.
- Spend additional time inside the museum or at the gold-leaf workshop.
- Carry small towels, umbrellas, and a change of socks for the child.

### Heavy rain

**Saturday:** Keep Omicho Market only if the family is comfortable with the walk from the bus stop. If not, have lunch near Kanazawa Station and go directly to the museum.

**Sunday:** Skip Kenrokuen and follow this indoor plan:

1. Check out and leave luggage at the hotel.
2. Travel to the gold-leaf workshop for the 11:00 session.
3. Have a relaxed lunch.
4. Return to the station by about 1:30 p.m.
5. Spend the remaining time resting at the station or, if the museum was skipped on Saturday, visit the museum instead.

## Decisions for the family

1. Does everyone agree that the main plan is better with four stops rather than adding Higashi Chaya?
2. Should all five people participate in the gold-leaf workshop?
3. Is a short taxi acceptable in an emergency, or should the group use buses only?
4. Which meals should be seafood-focused, and which restaurants can be reserved?
5. If the weather is poor, does the family prefer more museum time or more hotel and station rest?

## Confirm before booking

- Whether the hotel can hold luggage before check-in and after checkout.
- The hotel’s breakfast and checkout arrangements.
- The museum’s August 8 opening status, ticket availability, and the child’s admission category.
- The gold-leaf workshop’s exact price, available 11:00 time slot, and reservation conditions for five people.
- Restaurant reservations, especially Saturday dinner and the Omicho Market lunch.
- Kenrokuen identification requirements for the grandparents.
- Bus routes and service conditions during the travel week.
