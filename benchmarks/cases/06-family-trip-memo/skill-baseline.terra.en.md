# Kanazawa Family Trip Plan  
**Saturday–Sunday, August 8–9, 2026**

This plan keeps the days light: one main outing at a time, taxi travel between areas, and a seated break at least every 20 minutes of walking. We will prioritize Omicho Market, Kenrokuen, and one craft activity; the Higashi Chaya District and the 21st Century Museum are optional rather than commitments.

## Day 1 — Arrive, seafood lunch, rest, and crafts

Arrive at Kanazawa Station at 10:30 a.m. First, leave luggage with the hotel if it offers storage; this needs confirming. Take a taxi to Omicho Market rather than walking or waiting for a bus.

Have an early seafood lunch at the market, choosing a restaurant where the family can sit promptly. After lunch, browse only a small part of the market and stop for a drink or dessert before anyone is tired. The goal is lunch and atmosphere, not seeing every stall.

Around 1:30 p.m., take a taxi to the hotel area. If the room is not ready, rest in the lobby or a nearby café until the 3:00 p.m. check-in. Keep the afternoon break protected, especially for Grandfather and the child.

After resting, take a taxi to a pre-booked gold-leaf workshop. A workshop gives the child a hands-on activity and gives everyone a seated experience. If it is in or near Higashi Chaya District, spend no more than 15–20 minutes looking at the streets afterward, then take a taxi back to the station area for dinner.

Have dinner near Kanazawa Station, where returning to the hotel is simple. Choose seafood if the family still wants it, but keep the meal unhurried rather than treating it as another sightseeing stop.

## Day 2 — Garden first, then a flexible indoor option

After breakfast, take a taxi directly to Kenrokuen. Visit in short sections, using benches, tea houses, or shaded rest points before Grandfather reaches his walking limit. Aim for about 60–90 minutes in the garden, including breaks—not 60–90 minutes of continuous walking.

After Kenrokuen, decide based on the weather and everyone’s energy:

- If the weather is pleasant and the family still feels comfortable, have lunch nearby, then return by taxi to the hotel or station area for a final rest and an easy early meal before departure.
- If it rains, or if the garden visit feels like enough activity, take a taxi to the 21st Century Museum of Contemporary Art instead. Treat it as a short indoor visit with café or seating breaks, not a full museum day.
- If Grandfather is tired, skip the museum without concern and return to the station area early. A relaxed café, souvenir shopping close to the station, or an early lunch is a successful ending to the trip.

Collect luggage and be back at Kanazawa Station by about 3:45 p.m. for the 4:30 p.m. train.

## Rainy-weather version

On the first day, keep Omicho Market for lunch, check in and rest, then attend the gold-leaf workshop if booked. Skip or minimize the outdoor walk through Higashi Chaya District.

On the second day, replace Kenrokuen with the 21st Century Museum. If the museum is busy or unavailable, spend the day near Kanazawa Station with a long lunch, cafés, and covered shopping rather than forcing another destination.

## Budget guide — maximum ¥70,000 for five people

| Category | Family spending limit |
|---|---:|
| Meals and snacks | ¥44,000 |
| Activities, including garden admission and workshop | ¥14,000 |
| Local transport, mainly taxis | ¥10,000 |
| Unplanned expense reserve | ¥2,000 |
| **Total** | **¥70,000** |

These are spending limits rather than price estimates. If the gold-leaf workshop costs more than the activity allowance, reduce restaurant spending or choose the garden and market as the main paid activities.

## Before deciding, confirm

- Whether the hotel can store luggage before check-in and after check-out.
- Opening hours, admission prices, and accessibility information for Kenrokuen and the 21st Century Museum.
- Gold-leaf workshop availability, duration, price, location, and whether it needs advance reservation.
- Which Omicho Market restaurants can seat five people comfortably with minimal waiting.
- Taxi availability or a reliable alternative for each transfer.

The key family choice is whether to reserve the gold-leaf workshop. Reserving it gives the trip a clear craft experience; leaving it open makes the schedule more flexible but risks losing availability on a summer weekend.
