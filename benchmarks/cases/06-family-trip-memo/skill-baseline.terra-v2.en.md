# Kanazawa Family Trip Plan  
August 8–9, 2026

The aim is to enjoy Kanazawa without turning the weekend into a walking challenge. We should choose one main area at a time, use local transport between stops, and plan a seated break at least every 20 minutes of walking.

## Day 1: Market, hotel rest, and one easy afternoon activity

**10:30 a.m. — Arrive at Kanazawa Station**

Leave luggage at the hotel or use station storage, then travel directly to Omicho Market rather than walking there.

**11:00 a.m.–12:30 p.m. — Omicho Market lunch**

Have an early seafood lunch and browse only the sections that interest us most. This gives everyone a seat soon after arrival and avoids trying to visit the market during its busiest period.

**12:30–2:15 p.m. — Return toward Kanazawa Station and take a long break**

Have a café break near the station or hotel. This is intentionally unhurried: it gives Grandfather time to recover before check-in and gives the child some quiet time.

**3:00 p.m. — Hotel check-in and rest**

Rest in the room before going out again.

**4:30–6:00 p.m. — Gold-leaf workshop, if available**

A workshop is a good first-afternoon activity because it is seated, indoors, and suitable for both grandparents and a six-year-old. Choose a location reachable with minimal walking from a transport stop, and reserve only after confirming age suitability and availability.

**Evening — Dinner near Kanazawa Station**

Keep dinner close to the hotel. This avoids a second long journey and leaves room for an early night if needed.

## Day 2: Garden or museum, then an easy departure

**Morning — Choose one main destination**

Our first choice should be **Kenrokuen**, with transport to the nearest practical stop and a deliberately short route through the garden. We should not try to see every area. Plan to sit whenever possible and stop after roughly 15–20 minutes of walking.

Have an early lunch nearby or return toward the station, depending on everyone’s energy.

**Rainy-weather alternative: 21st Century Museum of Contemporary Art**

If the weather is wet, replace Kenrokuen with the museum. It provides an indoor activity with more opportunities to sit and rest. We should confirm which galleries are open and whether timed entry is needed before deciding.

**Early afternoon — Return to the hotel area**

Collect luggage, take a final café break, and leave plenty of time to reach the station without rushing.

**About 3:45 p.m. — Be at Kanazawa Station**

This allows time for toilets, snacks, luggage, and a calm boarding process before the 4:30 p.m. train.

## What we are leaving out

Higashi Chaya District is appealing, but adding it to this short trip would mean another area, more walking, and likely more waiting. We can include it only if the family feels energetic after the gold-leaf workshop, or substitute it for Kenrokuen—not add it on top.

## Budget guide: ¥70,000 total

These are spending limits for discussion, not confirmed prices.

| Category | Family budget |
|---|---:|
| Meals and snacks | ¥38,000 |
| Local transport, including taxis when walking would be difficult | ¥15,000 |
| Activities, including the workshop and museum or garden entry | ¥12,000 |
| Flexible reserve | ¥5,000 |
| **Total** | **¥70,000** |

Using taxis for the longest or most tiring transfers is worth protecting in the budget. The trip should prioritize comfort over squeezing in one more sight.

## Before we finalize

Confirm opening hours, admission prices, workshop reservations, accessibility, and the weather forecast. The key family decision is whether Day 2 should be the garden plan or the indoor museum plan; everything else should stay flexible enough to protect rest time.
